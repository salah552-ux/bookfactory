The answer is the Migrating Motor Complex. The MMC is your gut's built-in housekeeping system — a sweeping contraction that runs every 90 to 120 minutes between meals, clearing bacteria from the small intestine into the large intestine where they belong. When the MMC is disrupted — by post-infectious nerve damage, by hypothyroidism, by eating too frequently — bacteria aren't cleared. They accumulate. This is the gap that Fix Your Gut for Good was written to fill. Not a new protocol. The protocol behind the protocol — the one that addresses what your treatment probably didn't.

If you've cycled through two or more treatment rounds, you don't need another elimination diet. You need the mechanism. This is it.

*Amazon module type: Standard Single Right Image*

**Headline (118 characters):**
A four-phase protocol that treats relapse prevention as the main event, not a footnote.

**Body (2,104 characters):**
The 4-Phase Root Cause Framework is not a new diet. It's a sequence — and the sequence is the point.

**Phase 1 — Assessment**
Before treatment begins, the framework identifies which of three SIBO types is present: hydrogen-dominant, methane-dominant (IMO), or hydrogen sulfide. These behave differently and respond to different protocols. A hydrogen-dominant protocol applied to IMO fails. The Assessment phase also maps your specific root cause — the structural factor that created the conditions for overgrowth in the first place — across five categories: post-infectious MMC damage, connective tissue disorders, hypothyroidism, motility-suppressing medications, and chronic eating patterns.

**Phase 2 — Eradication**
Treatment sequenced to your specific type. For hydrogen-dominant SIBO: rifaximin alone. For IMO: rifaximin plus neomycin simultaneously (not sequentially). For hydrogen sulfide: bismuth-based protocols. The elemental diet is covered as a fallback when standard eradication stalls. Wrong sequence = predictable failure.

**Phase 3 — Repair**
Structured reintroduction. Not "when you feel better" — a specific sequence, starting with low-FODMAP foods one at a time on 72-hour intervals. The intestinal lining has been damaged. The gut bacteria balance is still re-establishing. Declaring victory before this process stabilises is the single most common cause of early relapse.

**Phase 4 — Root Cause Resolution**
MMC restoration: prokinetics (pharmaceutical and herbal, with evidence levels clearly stated), eating window optimisation, and management of the underlying condition — thyroid, connective tissue, post-infectious nerve damage — that caused the disruption. This is the phase that changes long-term outcomes. It is also the phase that almost no standard SIBO protocol includes.
