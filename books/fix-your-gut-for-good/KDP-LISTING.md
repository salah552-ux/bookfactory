## SECTION 2 — PEN NAME RECOMMENDATION

**Recommendation: Yes, use a pen name. Do not publish under a personal name unless the author holds a relevant clinical credential (MD, RD, ND, PhD in a relevant field).**

**Rationale:** Health and medical nonfiction on Amazon signals authority through author name conventions. Readers in the SIBO space are research-literate and skeptical — they have been failed by medical professionals and are hypersensitive to credential signals. A personal name with no visible credential will reduce trust. A pen name allows deliberate construction of authority positioning.

**Name type that converts in this category:**
- First name: real-feeling, not invented-sounding. Gender can be female (mirrors the primary reader) or neutral.
- Last name: strong, single syllable or two syllables, with a clinical-adjacent feel. Think: "Dr. James Holloway" or "Dr. Sarah Mercer" rather than "Dr. Wellness McHealthy."
- Credential display: if not using a real credential, do not invent one. Instead: "Researcher and health writer specializing in functional gut conditions" — specific enough to be credible, honest enough to be defensible.
- Do not use "Dr." unless the author holds a relevant doctorate. The FTC and Amazon both treat this as a material claim.

**Suggested pen name format:** [First Name] [Single-syllable Last Name], e.g., "Morgan Hale" or "Claire Reed." Clear, credible, gender-readable if desired, no forced credential.

## SECTION 8 — DISTRIBUTION DECISION

### Recommendation: KDP Select (90-day exclusive) — enroll at launch

**Rationale:**

1. **The audience is on Kindle Unlimited.** The SIBO reader is already a heavy Amazon health book consumer — research-literate, Kindle-native, and accustomed to KU for health content. KU borrows count toward BSR and trigger the "also bought" recommendation algorithm. A protocol book at this price point ($9.99 Kindle) will convert better under KU than as a purchase for readers who are cautious spenders after multiple failed treatments.

2. **Category rank acceleration.** The IBS/SIBO Kindle category has low enough competition that KU borrows in the first 30 days can push a new title into the top 10, generating organic visibility without paid ads. This is the fastest path to the recommendation algorithm for a new pen name with no existing author rank.

3. **Free promotion days.** KDP Select provides 5 free promotion days per 90-day term. A coordinated 2-day free promotion in week 3–4 (after initial reviews are established) can generate 500–2,000+ downloads in a health niche, seeding reviews and accelerating BSR in the SIBO category. This does not work on wide distribution.

4. **The SIBO reader does not use Kobo or Apple Books as their primary research tool.** Wide distribution matters for fiction readers and general nonfiction. The medically-researching gut health reader is on Amazon. Wide from day 1 would split already limited early traffic across platforms where the book has no rank, no reviews, and no discoverability.

5. **Re-evaluate at 90 days.** After the first KDP Select term, review sales velocity, KU page reads, and review count. If the book is generating 200+ reviews and strong organic rank, move to wide at Term 2 to capture residual demand on other platforms. If still building, renew KDP Select for a second term.

### Launch Price Strategy: Soft Launch at $7.99 eBook for Days 1–14, then Increase to $9.99
