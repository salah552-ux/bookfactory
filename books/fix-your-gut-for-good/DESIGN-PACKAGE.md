## 1. MARKET ANALYSIS

**What the top 5 SIBO/gut-health covers do visually (April 2026 Amazon snapshot).**
The SIBO bestseller shelf is dominated by two visual tribes. Tribe one: *wellness-soft* — *SIBO Made Simple*, *Healing SIBO*, *The SIBO Solution*. Sage green, terracotta, cream paper textures, hand-lettered script titles, botanicals, and a bowl of something neutral. These covers sell "gentle recovery." Tribe two: *clinical-cold* — *Super Gut*, *Fast Like a Girl*-adjacent titles, and newer Rodale/PRH imprints. White backgrounds, huge sans-serif title, a stock photo of a smiling woman, a thin red or blue accent bar. These covers sell "doctor said so." The direct relapse-lane competitor, *The SIBO Relapse Fix*, splits the difference badly: muted teal, a generic intestine illustration, and a subtitle crammed with four phrases. Thumbnails blur into each other.

**What is overused and now invisible.** Sage green. Hand-lettered scripts. Anatomical gut drawings. Cross-sections of the small intestine. Photos of leafy greens. The word "Healing" in a script face. Watercolor washes. Any cover where you cannot read the title at 160px. A 2026 KDP shelf scroll shows roughly 70% of the category converging on this look — meaning none of them are being *seen*.

**The specific visual gap this cover will own.** There is an open lane: *editorial-clinical authority* — the visual language of a serious investigative nonfiction hardback (*Empire of Pain*, *The Body Keeps the Score*, *How to Change Your Mind*) applied to a condition book. Typography-led, not imagery-led. Object-as-metaphor rather than anatomy. A single precise graphic device instead of illustration. Our cover will not show a gut. It will show a door closing — or more precisely, a typographic system that *behaves* like a closing loop — and it will do it in a color territory (deep ink navy + bone white + one accent) that no current SIBO title occupies.

**Color territories.** Saturated/invisible: sage green, terracotta, cream, pastel pink, teal, mustard. Available: deep ink navy (#0E1B2C), oxblood/brick (#7A1E1E), bone white (#F3EEE3), graphite charcoal (#1A1A1A), a single desaturated gold for accent (#C8A15A). The book uses deep ink navy as its anchor. This is the most authority-heavy available slot on the SIBO shelf in April 2026.

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION STATEMENT

**Concept (one sentence).** *Fix Your Gut for Good* looks like a piece of investigative nonfiction that happens to be a medical protocol — a typographic cover built around the word STOP as a hard visual object, signaling precision and finality rather than wellness and hope.

**Mood — 3 specific reference points.**
1. *Empire of Pain* (Patrick Radden Keefe, Doubleday hardback) — typographic dominance, deep ink color, institutional weight.
2. *Why We Sleep* (Matthew Walker, Scribner paperback) — large readable title, subtitle treated as a clinical claim not a tagline, authority without sterility.
3. *The Checklist Manifesto* (Atul Gawande, Metropolitan Books) — the sense that a system is being handed to you in physical form; title as object.

**Color palette (with rationale).**
- **Ink Navy #0E1B2C** — background field, 70% of the cover. Reads as authority, not sadness. Prints cleanly in CMYK without muddying. Owns an empty lane on the SIBO shelf.
- **Bone White #F3EEE3** — primary title color. Warmer than pure white, which prevents the cover from tipping into "pharmaceutical label." This is the "human warmth" half of the brand position.
- **Oxblood #7A1E1E** — single accent. Used only on the word "RELAPSING" (struck through) and on the thin rule above the subtitle. Clinical red, not alarm red. Says *this is the problem* without screaming.
- **Desaturated Gold #C8A15A** — tertiary, used only for author name. Warm editorial accent; prevents the cover reading as purely monochrome.

**Compositional approach — where the eye goes.**
1. **First (2 sec):** The word **STOP** in a dense, slightly condensed serif display at ~38% of cover height, top third. Unmissable at 160px.
2. **Second (4 sec):** **RELAPSING** directly beneath, same weight, with a single oxblood horizontal rule struck through it — literally "stop relapsing" performed typographically.
3. **Third (7 sec):** The subtitle's first line — *"THE 4-PHASE SIBO PROTOCOL"* — in clinical small-caps, centered, with "SIBO" the largest word in the subtitle so it is legible at thumbnail.
4. **Fourth (10 sec):** "S.A. Ibrahim" in desaturated gold at the base.

**Typography treatment.**
- Title: **Libre Baskerville Bold** at display size, tight tracking (-20), ~38% cover height. Tested: readable as "FIX YOUR GUT FOR GOOD" at 160px.
- Subtitle: **Libre Baskerville Small Caps** at ~6% cover height, letter-spacing +80, two-line maximum. "SIBO" set one size larger than surrounding words.
- Author: **Libre Baskerville Regular**, small caps, gold, ~3.5% cover height.
- Interior body (referenced here for consistency): **EB Garamond 11pt**.

**What the cover is NOT doing.**
- Not showing any anatomy, cross-section, intestine, or gut illustration.
- Not using sage green, terracotta, or cream backgrounds.
- Not using script or hand-lettered type anywhere.
- Not showing photography of food, a person, or a bowl.
- Not putting the word "Healing" anywhere on the cover.
- Not performing hope.

### 3c. Iteration Guide

If the first generation is 80% correct, refine with these specific edits rather than regenerating:

1. **Title too small at thumbnail.** Edit: "Increase the word STOP and the word RELAPSING to fill 92% of the cover width; reduce top margin to 6%."
2. **Strike-through rule too thick / looks cartoonish.** Edit: "Reduce the oxblood horizontal rule through RELAPSING to a precise 2px line, editorial not graphic, contained strictly within the letterform baseline."
3. **SIBO illegible at thumbnail.** Edit: "Set the word SIBO two point sizes larger than the surrounding subtitle words, bolder weight, and letter-space the rest of the subtitle wider to emphasize contrast."
4. **Navy reads as black.** Edit: "Lift the background to #0E1B2C exactly — warmer, slightly blue-shifted; ensure it is visibly navy not black when viewed against a pure black reference."
5. **Gold reads as yellow.** Edit: "Desaturate the gold to #C8A15A — an editorial dull brass, not a bright metallic yellow."
6. **Looks too 'designed' / decorative.** Edit: "Remove all texture, gradient, and shadow effects. The cover must read as flat editorial print, not a digital illustration."
7. **Subtitle wraps awkwardly.** Edit: "Force the subtitle into exactly two lines: line 1 'THE 4-PHASE SIBO PROTOCOL', line 2 'FOR ROOT CAUSE RECOVERY'."

### 3d. 160px Thumbnail Readability Test

**Explicit statement.** At 160px cover width:
- "FIX YOUR GUT FOR GOOD" — **readable.** This is the primary reason the title occupies nearly 40% of cover height and uses high-contrast Libre Baskerville Bold on a near-black navy field.
- "SIBO" — **readable**, because it is deliberately set one size larger than its subtitle neighbors and appears on its own pronounced line. This satisfies the KDP-LISTING.md Section 1 requirement: *"Ensure 'SIBO' is visible in the first line of the subtitle at thumbnail scale."*
- "The 4-Phase Protocol for Root Cause Recovery" — **not fully readable** at 160px, and this is intentional. Nothing in the subtitle beyond SIBO needs to read at thumbnail. The reader clicks because "FIX YOUR GUT FOR GOOD" + "SIBO" told them what the book is about in 400 milliseconds.
- Author name "S.A. IBRAHIM" — **not readable** at 160px. This is correct; author name is tertiary for a debut pen name.

## 5. CHAPTER HEADER RECOMMENDATION

**Option 1 — Minimal Editorial.**
Chapter number in Roman numerals (I, II, III...) in Libre Baskerville small caps 10pt oxblood, centered. Beneath: chapter title in Libre Baskerville Bold 18pt, centered, max two lines. Thin oxblood 1" rule beneath, centered. 1/3 page drop before body text.

**Option 2 — Clinical Plate.**
"CHAPTER [N]" in Libre Baskerville small caps 9pt oxblood, flush-left. Beneath: chapter title in Libre Baskerville Bold 20pt, flush-left, max two lines. Beneath: a one-sentence chapter thesis (the "Core Argument" from BLUEPRINT.md) in EB Garamond italic 11pt, flush-left, max 20 words. Then body text.

**Option 3 — Phase-Aware (RECOMMENDED).**
A two-tier header that encodes the book's most important structural claim: that chapters belong to phases, and phases are the point.

- Line 1: **PHASE [TWO] · ERADICATION** in Libre Baskerville small caps 9pt oxblood, letter-spaced +120, centered
- Line 2: thin oxblood horizontal rule, 0.75" wide, centered, 6pt below Line 1
- Line 3: **CHAPTER [5]** in Libre Baskerville small caps 9pt bone-black, centered, 12pt below the rule
- Line 4: **The Treatment Protocol** in Libre Baskerville Bold 22pt, centered, max two lines, 8pt below Line 3
- Line 5: Core Argument one-liner in EB Garamond italic 11pt, centered, max 18 words, 14pt below Line 4
- 1/3 page drop before body

**Why Option 3.** The entire marketing and editorial thesis of this book — stated verbatim in KDP-LISTING.md and BLUEPRINT.md — is that *the 4-phase structure is what makes this book different from every other SIBO protocol*. If the chapter header doesn't name the phase, the book's structural claim evaporates the moment the reader is inside a chapter. Option 3 also means that every chapter quietly reinforces the 4-phase map the reader is being taught to own (Turning Point 3: "passive patient → informed director"). Options 1 and 2 are cleaner typographically, but Option 3 is load-bearing for the book's argument. Cost: a few extra lines of space at chapter openings. Worth it.

Carryover benefit: Option 3 is also the most scalable to Books 2–10 in the series. Each future Reflex Press condition-book will have its own phase scheme, and Option 3 gives every future book the same chapter-header scaffolding to express it.
