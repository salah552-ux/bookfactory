Hi,

Most SIBO appointments end the same way: the doctor tells you what your test showed, explains the treatment, and says to come back if it doesn't work.

What doesn't get asked — almost ever — is why the bacteria are there.

Not "what is growing in your small intestine." That's the diagnosis. The question that changes the outcome is: what created the conditions that allowed bacteria to colonise the small intestine in the first place, and is that condition still present?

Because if the root cause is still present — if the MMC is still disrupted, if the thyroid is still borderline, if the eating pattern is still interrupting the cleaning cycle every 90 minutes — then the bacteria will return. Not because the treatment failed. Because the door was left open.

The question to bring to your next appointment is this:

*"I'd like to understand what root cause we're addressing alongside the antibiotic treatment. What's driving the overgrowth, and what specific steps are we taking to address it?"*

Phrase it exactly like that. It's specific enough that it requires a specific answer. It signals you've read the research. It gives a clinician who's thinking along the same lines an opening to work with you properly — and it gives you a clear signal if they haven't.

If they don't have an answer, Chapter 10 of the book has a full case summary template and a set of DCT scripts for exactly that conversation.
