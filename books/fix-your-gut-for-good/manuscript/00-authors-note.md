# A Note Before We Begin
## Why I Wrote This Book

It started in 2011.

Not with a diagnosis. With a disappearance. The person I had been before — someone who ate without thinking, who didn't plan their day around bathroom proximity, who didn't lie awake calculating what they'd eaten and why it felt like something was rotting inside them — that person just stopped showing up.

What showed up instead was a body I didn't recognise. Bloating so severe I looked pregnant by afternoon. A brain that fogged over by mid-morning and didn't lift until I hadn't eaten for hours. Fatigue that sleep couldn't touch. A nervous system that ran like a frayed wire — not anxious the way people get anxious, not worried about something. Just wrong. Humming at the wrong frequency. All the time.

I went to doctors. Of course I did.

They ran tests. The tests came back normal. They told me it was IBS. They told me to manage my stress. They told me to try a low-FODMAP diet. One of them, who I had waited three months to see, spent eleven minutes with me and wrote *functional bowel disorder* on a notepad like that meant something. It meant: we don't know, and we're done looking.

I was not done looking.

What followed was fifteen years of research that started out of desperation and turned into something I didn't expect: clarity. I read the studies. I found the researchers who were asking the right questions. I learned what the migrating motor complex was before most gastroenterologists were talking about it in clinical practice. I understood the connection between a food poisoning episode years earlier and what was happening in my small intestine now. I learned that there were three distinct types of overgrowth, each requiring different treatment, and that most breath tests only measured one. I learned that I had spent years being treated for a label — IBS — that was simply a description of symptoms with the cause left blank.

The cause was not left blank. It had a name. It had a mechanism. It had an explanation for every symptom I had been told were unrelated — the brain fog, the anxiety, the skin flares, the joint pain that moved around and never showed up on imaging. None of those were separate problems. They were one problem, broadcasting across every system in my body because the system responsible for absorption, for serotonin production, for histamine clearance, for immune regulation, had been compromised for years.

Nobody told me this. I had to find it myself.

This book is what I found.

I am not a doctor. I am not writing to you as someone who has treated patients or run clinical trials. I am writing to you as someone who spent fifteen years doing what you are probably doing right now — reading everything, cross-referencing everything, lying awake trying to understand why the official answers keep not working. I got to the bottom of it. I followed the research to where it actually leads, not where it stops when the appointment ends.

Everything in this book is sourced. Every statistic, every mechanism, every claim has a reference trail you can follow. I have been rigorous because I had to be — I needed to know this was real before I could trust it.

You don't have to spend fifteen years getting here. That's the only reason I wrote this down.

The path is specific. The mechanism is real. The reason you're still in it isn't that you're broken or that medicine has nothing left to offer you — it's that nobody has shown you the full picture yet.

That changes in the next chapter.

— *S.A. Ibrahim*
