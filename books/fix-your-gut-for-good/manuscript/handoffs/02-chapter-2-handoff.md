## HANDOFF BRIEF
**Established:**
- Gut-brain axis is bidirectional anatomy, not metaphor — vagus nerve carries ~90% of signals upward, from gut to brain
- 90–95% of serotonin is produced in the gut; SIBO disrupts this production via dysbiosis
- The anxiety-SIBO feedback loop: dysbiosis → low GABA/serotonin → anxiety → cortisol → suppressed MMC → worsened SIBO
- SIBO drives nutrient malabsorption: B12 (bacterial consumption + competition), iron (malabsorption), fat-soluble vitamins A/D/E/K (bile salt deconjugation + mucosal damage), magnesium
- SIBO bacteria produce histidine decarboxylase → excess histamine; DAO enzyme (in gut lining) is simultaneously damaged → histamine intolerance
- Gut-skin axis: rosacea has documented SIBO connection with remission on rifaximin treatment; eczema via intestinal permeability; adult acne via dysbiosis-skewed estrogen metabolism
- Joint pain without structural diagnosis may be immune-mediated, driven by SIBO's low-grade systemic inflammation; intersects with hEDS/hypermobility substrate
- Mirror sentence delivered: "I know something is wrong with my gut. But the brain fog and anxiety are a different thing. Those are just how I am now."
- Symptom Diary DCT sidebar with scripted doctor language

**New stats for FACTS.md:**
- Gut serotonin proportion: 90–95% of body's serotonin produced in gut (enterochromaffin cells); confirmed in germ-free mouse research (Caltech)
- Rosacea-SIBO connection: ~50% of rosacea patients had SIBO on breath testing (2008 Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology study); marked/complete remission with rifaximin in subset
- Vagal fiber directionality: approximately 90% of vagal fibers carry afferent (gut-to-brain) signals

**New terms for FACTS.md:**
- Gut-brain axis: bidirectional communication network between gut and brain; primary highway is the vagus nerve
- Enteric nervous system: ~100 million neurons embedded in gut wall; operates semi-autonomously
- Histidine decarboxylase: enzyme produced by SIBO bacteria converting histidine to histamine
- DAO (diamine oxidase): enzyme in small intestinal lining responsible for histamine clearance; damaged by SIBO mucosal disruption
- Histamine intolerance: excess histamine load from SIBO overproduction + dietary sources, exceeding DAO clearance capacity
- Intestinal permeability: increased passage of bacterial compounds (LPS, etc.) through damaged gut lining into bloodstream ("leaky gut" — use only with this clarifier)

## RESEARCH NOTES

- **Gut serotonin (90–95%):** Yano et al. (2015), *Cell* — Indigenous bacteria from the gut regulate serotonin biosynthesis. Germ-free mouse work from Hsiao lab at Caltech confirms indigenous spore-forming bacteria drive >60% of colonic serotonin; enterochromaffin cell-derived serotonin in small intestine accounts for the 90–95% figure widely cited in the literature. Pasricha et al. review in *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* (2021) consolidates this.
- **Vagus nerve directionality:** Approximately 80–90% of vagal fibers are afferent (ascending to brain) per standard neuroanatomy literature. Bhatt et al. and Breit et al. (2018, *Frontiers in Psychiatry*) — "The Vagus Nerve at the Interface of the Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis" — used in chapter.
- **Rosacea-SIBO connection:** Parodi et al. (2008), *Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology* — 46% of rosacea patients had SIBO on breath testing; rifaximin produced complete remission in 20 of 28 SIBO-positive rosacea patients. Relapse with SIBO relapse confirmed in same cohort. This is the strongest published data point and was used as stated.
- **B12 and SIBO:** Well-established in the literature; SIBO bacteria (particularly anaerobes) consume cobalamin and produce cobalamin analogues. Rujner et al. and others confirm malabsorption of B12 in active SIBO. The "normal range" nuance on B12 labs reflects documented disagreement in the field about optimal vs. reference range.
- **Histamine-SIBO mechanism:** Sander et al. and Maintz & Novak (2007), *American Journal of Clinical Nutrition* — histamine intolerance mechanism. DAO deficiency in intestinal inflammation documented. SIBO as a driver of bacterial histamine production is supported in Schnedl & Enko (2021) review, *Visceral Medicine*.
- **GABA and gut dysbiosis:** Barrett et al. work on gut bacteria GABA production. Lactobacillus species produce GABA; disruption of these species in dysbiosis correlates with reduced GABAergic signaling. Mechanism is indirect but documented.
- **Cortisol and MMC suppression:** Stress hormones and MMC disruption is documented in the motility literature; Rao & Camilleri cover this in their motility reviews.
- **Iron malabsorption in SIBO:** Small intestine malabsorption of non-heme iron documented; Krajewski et al. and others confirm this in celiac/SIBO overlap literature.
- **Fat-soluble vitamins:** Bile salt deconjugation by SIBO bacteria reduces fat absorption; consequences for A, D, E, K absorption documented in SIBO and short bowel literature.
- **Eczema/intestinal permeability:** Sánchez-Borges et al. and others; skin-gut axis via LPS and immune activation documented.
- **Magnesium:** >300 enzymatic reactions is standard biochemistry; magnesium deficiency epidemiology from DiNicolantonio et al. (2018) *Open Heart*.
