## CURRENT AMAZON REVIEW GUIDELINES

WebSearch was available and was used this session. Key rules confirmed against current (2026) KDP / Amazon Community guidance:

- **Authors may ask readers to leave a review.** A simple back-matter request for a review is standard, accepted practice. It is not the prohibited conduct.
- **You may NOT incentivise a review.** Offering anything beyond a free or discounted copy of the book itself — gift cards, gifts, money, services — invalidates the review and gets it removed.
- **You may NOT require a review in exchange for a free/discounted copy**, and may NOT attempt to influence what the review says.
- **You may NOT ask specifically for positive / 5-star reviews.** The ask must be for an honest account only.
- **No review-trading, no soliciting friends/family, no manipulation.** Reviews must be authentic and uninfluenced.
- One source-tier nuance worth flagging: some 2026 commentary argues even the phrase "please leave an honest review" is treated cautiously by Amazon when it reads as a generic solicitation. The defensible position the rewritten CTA takes: ask for the reader's *honest experience* in the reader's own words, framed around helping the next reader, with explicitly **nothing offered in return** — which satisfies both the strict standing rules and the cautious reading.

Standing rules applied regardless of source nuance: (a) authors MAY ask for an honest review; (b) NO incentive, NO exchange, NO request for positive/5-star; (c) ask only for honest experience.

## OPTIMIZED BACK MATTER SEQUENCE (APPLIED)

Inserted in `99-back-matter.md` after the "Also in the Series" block, under a new heading **"Before You Close the Book,"** as the final pages of the book:

**Beat 1 — Acknowledgment:**
> "You came to this book with a gut that had been shouting for a long time, and you spent four weeks learning to turn the alarm down five minutes at a time. Whatever your tracker says today — a big drop, a quiet one, or simply more ordinary days than rough ones — you did the work most people never get around to. You trained a nerve you didn't know you had."

**Beat 2 — The ask:**
> "There's one small thing that helps the next person, and it costs you a couple of minutes. Somewhere out there is another bloated, exhausted reader scrolling through the same crowded shelf you once stood in front of, trying to work out which of these books is honest and which is two hundred pages of jargon with no real exercises. Your review is what helps them decide. If this workbook earned its place for you, a few honest sentences on Amazon — what it was actually like to use, what changed, what didn't — is the most direct way to put it in front of someone who needs it. Say exactly what you found; an honest account helps far more than a glowing one."

**Beat 3 — Forward motion:**
> "That's it. No upsell, no catch — the same deal as the whole book. Keep your stack going, come back to Chapter 4 when life gets busy, and look after that nerve. It's quietly looking after you."

**Voice check:** Matches the book's established register — second person, contractions, dry-warm, "crowded shelf / two hundred pages of jargon" echoes the Introduction's own complaint about competitor books; "look after that nerve. It's quietly looking after you" mirrors Chapter 4's closing line. No "as an author, reviews help me." No effusiveness the dry voice wouldn't use.

**Compliance of final CTA:** **PASS.** Asks only for an honest account ("say exactly what you found; an honest account helps far more than a glowing one" actively steers AWAY from positive-only). Offers nothing in return; explicitly states "no upsell, no catch." Frames around helping the next reader, not author benefit. No incentive, no exchange.

**Second-location fix (`14-appendices.md`):** The duplicate CTA was removed from under the free-audio QR and replaced with a compliant pointer ("there's a short note at the very back of the book about one quiet way you can help the next reader") plus an explicit "nothing is asked of you in return" on the free-audio line. This removes the perceived incentive adjacency and consolidates the actual ask into the single, optimized back-matter sequence.
