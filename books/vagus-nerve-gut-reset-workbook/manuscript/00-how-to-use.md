# How to Use This Workbook

## A Two-Minute Orientation Before You Begin

You can read this workbook straight through, front to back, and it will walk you from your first nervous, bloated day to a calmer gut twenty-eight days later. That is how it is built — as a path, one short day at a time.

But you might not be in a reading mood. You might be bloated right now, or mid-flare, or simply tired of health books that bury one good idea under forty pages of theory. So here is the shortcut.

If your main problem is your gut — bloating, cramping, the unpredictable IBS kind of misery — you can skim Week 1 and Week 2 and go straight to **Week 3**. That week is built for you. It is the reason this book exists.

If you mostly can't switch off at night, **Week 2** is yours.

If stress is the thing that sets everything off, **Week 4** is where you'll live.

But here is my honest advice: do the weeks in order if you can. Each one teaches a skill the next one uses. By the time you reach the gut week, the breathing will already feel like yours.

A word on what this workbook will and won't do. It will explain the mechanism behind every exercise, briefly, because you deserve to know *why* you're humming at your kitchen sink, not just be told to. It will cite real, published science — you'll find every source listed at the back. It will never sell you a supplement, a tea, a device, or a course. Not once.

Here's the rhythm you'll settle into. Each of the four weeks has a theme — foundations, sleep, the gut itself, then making it stick. Inside each week are seven dated daily pages. A page takes about five minutes to *do* and twenty seconds to *fill in*, and that's the whole daily commitment. You don't read ahead, you don't prepare, you don't need anything you don't already own. You turn to today's page, do what it says, jot a few quick notes, and get on with your life. Tomorrow you turn the page.

The tracking is not optional homework — it's part of how the program works. Every day you'll rate your gut and your stress in the moment, note one thing you noticed, and write a one-line win. At the end of each week you'll pull those numbers into a small table and compare. At the very end, you'll re-score the baseline you set at the start. That before-and-after, in your own handwriting, is what turns a vague sense of "I think this is helping" into proof. It's also, frankly, what keeps you going on the days you'd rather not.

What it won't do is diagnose you. Persistent gut symptoms need a doctor's eyes to rule out anything serious. This workbook is what you do *alongside* good medical care, not instead of it. Think of it as five minutes a day that works on the one part of your gut most treatments ignore: the nerve that runs it.

You need a pen, five minutes, and a willingness to notice what your body has been telling you for years. Let's begin.
