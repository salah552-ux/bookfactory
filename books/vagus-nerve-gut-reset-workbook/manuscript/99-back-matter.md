# Sources & Further Reading

Every load-bearing claim in this book is drawn from published, peer-reviewed science. The key sources are listed below so you can check them yourself — something the books this one competes with too often skip.

**On vagus nerve anatomy and function**
- Berthoud HR, Neuhuber WL. Functional and chemical anatomy of the afferent vagal system. *Autonomic Neuroscience*, 2000.
- Agostoni E, et al. Functional and histological studies of the vagus nerve and its branches. *Journal of Physiology*, 1957. *(Source of the ~80% afferent / ~20% efferent fibre split.)*

**On the gut-brain axis**
- Mayer EA. Gut feelings: the emerging biology of gut–brain communication. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 2011.
- Cryan JF, et al. The Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis. *Physiological Reviews*, 2019.
- Furness JB. The enteric nervous system and neurogastroenterology. *Nature Reviews Gastroenterology & Hepatology*, 2012. *(Source of the 200–600 million enteric neurons figure.)*
- Bonaz B, Bazin T, Pellissier S. The Vagus Nerve at the Interface of the Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis. *Frontiers in Neuroscience*, 2018.

**On the vagus and inflammation**
- Tracey KJ. The inflammatory reflex. *Nature*, 2002. *(The cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway — the "brake.")*

**On IBS as a disorder of gut-brain interaction**
- Drossman DA. Functional Gastrointestinal Disorders: History, Pathophysiology, Clinical Features, and Rome IV. *Gastroenterology*, 2016.
- Travagli RA, Anselmi L. Vagal neurocircuitry and its influence on gastric motility. *Nature Reviews Gastroenterology & Hepatology*, 2016.

**On the breathing exercises**
- Balban MY, et al. Brief structured respiration practices enhance mood and reduce physiological arousal. *Cell Reports Medicine*, 2023. *(The physiological sigh trial.)*
- Lehrer PM, Gevirtz R. Heart rate variability biofeedback: how and why does it work? *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2014.
- Russo MA, et al. The physiological effects of slow breathing in the healthy human. *Breathe*, 2017.

**On sound, cold, and movement**
- Weitzberg E, Lundberg JON. Humming greatly increases nasal nitric oxide. *American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine*, 2002.
- Kalyani BG, et al. Neurohemodynamic correlates of 'OM' chanting: a pilot fMRI study. *International Journal of Yoga*, 2011. *(A small pilot — early evidence.)*
- Jungmann M, et al. Effects of Cold Stimulation on Cardiac-Vagal Activation in Healthy Participants. *JMIR Formative Research*, 2018.
- Laborde S, Mosley E, Thayer JF. Heart Rate Variability and Cardiac Vagal Tone in Psychophysiological Research. *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2017.

A note on the science: where a finding comes from a small pilot study or an animal model rather than a large human trial, this book says so plainly in the text and calls it "early evidence." The exercises here are low-risk and grounded in real physiology, but no honest book pretends every mechanism is fully settled. Yours isn't a lab — it's your own body, and your own tracker is the most relevant evidence you'll ever collect.
