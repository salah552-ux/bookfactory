## 1. Concept (one sentence)
A deep-navy clinical-calm cover that extends the locked *Fix Your Gut for Good*
series DNA (navy / cream / gold) while owning the one lane both indie leaders

## 2. Market analysis — what is winning visually in this niche

Source: WebSearch sweep of Amazon vagus nerve / somatic workbook covers (2026-06-20)
plus the harvest data in COMPETITIVE-INTELLIGENCE.md and the blueprint cover brief (§10).

**What the top-selling vagus nerve workbook covers do (the homogeneous field):**
- **Colour territory:** the category is saturated with calming **teal, sky-blue,
  and sage-green** backgrounds — the default "nervous-system calm" signal. Like
  the gut-health shelf, this means the category is visually invisible against
  itself in a search grid. (Bennett, Nicholson, Benson, Walters, the Somatic
  Exercises titles all sit in this blue/teal/sage band.)
- **Imagery pattern:** soft botanicals, abstract "calm" gradients, brain-and-body
  line silhouettes, lotus/leaf motifs, occasional anatomical vagus-nerve line
  diagrams. The "Healing Bible" tier leans minimalist-illustrated.
- **Typography:** light/medium rounded sans-serif, usually centred, pale text on
  the calm ground. Reads approachable but lightweight — no authority signal.
- **The number/time hook is universal:** 10-Minute, 4-Week, 28-Day, 30-Day. Every
  competitor leads with a number. This is table stakes — our cover must too.
- **Payne ("The Vagus Nerve Protocol"):** protocol-positioned, calm palette,
  prose-anchor look (not a workbook aesthetic).
- **Bennett ("Vagus Nerve Reset Workbook"):** trauma/anxiety-positioned, calm
  blue-teal workbook look, "befriend your nervous system" register — **zero gut
  signalling on the cover.**

**The visual gap this cover owns:**
1. **Deep navy, not teal/sage.** Per the series DNA, our navy ground is already a
   hard pattern-interrupt against the entire blue-teal-sage wellness shelf — the
   same move that worked for Books 1 and 3 on the gut shelf works double here.
2. **Explicit gut-brain signalling.** Neither leader shows the gut. A calm line
   motif connecting a brain/vagus line down to a gut/belly form instantly
   telegraphs the gut-brain-axis angle that is our entire differentiator.
3. **Authority + workbook signalling together.** Cream serif title (authority) +
   a faint daily-log grid texture (real workbook, counters the "not a real
   workbook" complaint) — neither competitor does both.

**Why teal for Book 4 (and only as the small accent):** a restrained, desaturated
teal is the single nod to the nervous-system-calm semantics the niche expects —
but kept to the motif and one lane label, *not* the background. The navy ground
still owns the thumb-stop; the teal accent earns category recognition without
joining the invisible teal-background crowd. This keeps the series DNA intact
(navy/cream/gold dominant) while giving Book 4 its own identity, exactly as
Books 1 and 3 each took a different warm accent.

## 4. Composition (where the eye goes)
1. **Time hook "4-WEEK"** — large, high, gold or cream. Every competitor leads
   with a number; ours is the dial of the motif so it reads first at thumbnail.
2. **"VAGUS NERVE"** — largest cream serif keyword block; the search term the
   right reader is typing.
3. **"GUT RESET"** — completes the title (no "WORKBOOK" — this is a prose book; format/genre is now signalled by the "DAILY 5-MINUTE EXERCISES" badge, the "4-WEEK" hook, and the faint daily-log grid texture, NOT by the word "workbook").
4. **Gut-brain nerve-line motif** (centre) — a single clean cream/teal line from a
   small brain/head form curving down the body to a calm gut/belly form; the
   visual proof of the gut-brain-axis angle no competitor shows.
5. **"CALM THE GUT-BRAIN AXIS"** (teal lane) — the differentiator line.
6. **Badge "DAILY 5-MINUTE EXERCISES"** — small gold sticker-style callout,
   telegraphs depth + the low-friction daily promise (counters thin-content fear).
7. Series brand (top) + author in gold (bottom) — series recognition + trust.

## 5. Typography
- **Title / subtitle:** **Libre Baskerville Bold** — series-locked (matches Book 3),
  editorial authority, verified legible at 100px. Title all-caps, stacked.
- **"4-WEEK" + badge:** heavy condensed sans-serif (Bebas Neue / Oswald) for the
  number hook contrast — same device family as Book 1's numeral.
- No script, no thin weights, no italics in the title block.
- Title must pass the 100px squint test: "VAGUS NERVE" decodable at thumbnail.

## 6. What this cover is NOT doing
No teal/sage/sky-blue background (accent only), no soft wellness gradient, no lotus
or generic botanical, no anxious-person stock photo, no realistic brain or
intestine anatomy, no food photography, no probiotic bottles, no pastel, no
"Complete Guide"/"Beginners", no "Heal/Cure" verb in title position, no script
fonts, no author photo, no imprint, no AI-clutter. Avoids the entire genre-default
calm-teal-background trap and the COVER-PSYCHOLOGY trap list.

## 9. KDP technical spec reminder
- **eBook cover:** 1600 × 2560 px, portrait, aspect ratio 1:1.6, JPEG, sRGB, 3
  channels (no alpha — transparency causes rejection), under 50 MB.
- Design at 300 DPI; export web JPEG for the eBook.
- **Paperback:** 6 × 9 in trim, 0.125 in bleed all sides, 0.25 in safe zone,
  300 DPI, CMYK, single PDF (front + spine + back). Spine width = page count ×
  0.0025 in (cream paper); confirm against KDP cover calculator before placing
  spine text.
- After generation: resize to exactly 1600 × 2560 JPEG and run the 8-point Sharp
  compliance check (format, width, height, colour space, channels, alpha, file
  size, ratio) — all 8 must pass before the human gate.

## 10. Design decisions the Architect should know
1. **Series cover palette ≠ interior palette.** The locked-style memory file gives
   the *interior* navy `#1b3a5c` and tan `#c8b99a`. The shipped Book 1 & Book 3
   *covers* use a darker navy `#0E1B2C` + cream `#F3EEE3` + gold `#C8A15A`. This
   brief follows the shipped covers for shelf consistency. Flag if you want the
   memory updated to record the two-tier (cover vs interior) palette.
2. **Book 4 warm accent = muted teal `#3E9B8E`.** Books rotate their accent
   (Book 1 terracotta/oxblood, Book 3 ochre). Teal is chosen because it nods to
   the nervous-system-calm niche semantics without joining the invisible
   teal-background crowd — it stays an accent only.
3. **Gut-brain motif is the wedge.** Both indie leaders (Payne, Bennett) show zero
   gut signalling — Bennett is trauma-positioned, Payne is protocol-positioned.
   The gut-brain line motif is the cheapest, clearest way to claim the open lane
   the COMPETITIVE-INTELLIGENCE file identifies as our primary differentiator.
4. **DALL-E text caveat.** DALL-E/ChatGPT image gen renders long text imperfectly.
   Recommend generating the navy ground + gut-brain motif as the art, then setting
   the actual title/subtitle/badge typographically in an HTML cover render (as
   done for Book 1 `cover-kooky.html` and Book 3 `cover.html`) for guaranteed
   crisp, legible, brand-correct type. The prompt above requests full text for a
   one-shot option, but the HTML-type route is the reliable path to 7/7 on Q1/Q5.
