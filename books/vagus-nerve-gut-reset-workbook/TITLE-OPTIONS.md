## TOP 3 RECOMMENDATION

### #1 (PRIMARY) — The Vagus Nerve Gut Reset
*Calm the Gut-Brain Axis and Ease Bloating, IBS & Anxiety in 4 Weeks — A Plan of Daily 5-Minute Exercises to Reset Your Nervous System*

**Score: 46/50** · **Approach:** Outcome-Specific · **Primary keyword:** vagus nerve gut reset (title) + gut-brain axis, IBS, bloating (subtitle)

**The case:** This is the smallest possible edit from the rejected title that completely removes the rejection trigger. Dropping the single word "Workbook" turns a flagged blank-book title into a prose title, while preserving the brand recognition, the locked series fit, and the exact keyword string ("Vagus Nerve Gut Reset") the listing, cover, and series metadata were already built around. It wins on all five dimensions at once: maximum searchability (the title contains the verbatim primary keyword and the subtitle carries every opportunity keyword — gut-brain axis, IBS, bloating — flagged in BLUEPRINT §6); strong clickability and emotion (it names the IBS/bloating sufferer's exact problem rather than the generic "anxiety/sleep" promise every competitor uses); and best-in-class differentiation, because it fuses the proven prose-program framing (Payne's "Protocol"/"Plan" lane ranks — BSR #118,524 / 352 reviews / 4.7★, source: COMPETITIVE-INTELLIGENCE.md) with the one gap no ranking competitor fills: substantiated gut/IBS depth. The subtitle phrase "A Plan of Daily 5-Minute Exercises" is what makes it unambiguously a readable guide, not a fill-in book — it describes content the reader will *read and do*, not blanks they fill. The "4 Weeks" + "5-Minute" hooks satisfy the table-stakes time/number convention without leaning on them as the differentiator.

**Risk:** Retains "Reset," which is common in the niche — but uniqueness comes from the GUT pairing, not the verb. Confirm at upload that no banned-format word survives anywhere in metadata, since the rejection was format-triggered. The book's manuscript is already prose-converting; the title promises a "Plan of Exercises," which the 12 named exercises (FACTS.md) and 4-week structure fully support — no over-promise.

**Cover typography note:** Set "GUT RESET" largest; "THE VAGUS NERVE" as a smaller kicker above it. Float the "4-WEEK" / "5-MINUTE" number hook as a high badge. Subtitle in restrained sans beneath. Must read at 200px thumbnail (BLUEPRINT §10).

### Alternative 1 — The Gut-Brain Reset Protocol
*A 4-Week Vagus Nerve Plan to Calm IBS, Bloating & Anxiety with Daily 5-Minute Exercises*

**Score: 42/50** · **Approach:** Authority / Protocol
**Use if:** the Architect wants the strongest possible *prose-authority* signal and is willing to demote "vagus nerve" from the main title to the subtitle. "Protocol" is the single most directly KDP-proven prose word in this exact niche (it is Payne's ranking title). This leads with the gut-brain differentiator in the main title and reads as a definitive authored system — zero blank-book risk. Trade-off: it sacrifices the verbatim "vagus nerve [...] reset" search string in the main title (search 8 vs 10), and breaks byte-match with the existing series/listing metadata, requiring those to be re-locked.

### Alternative 2 — Why Your Gut Won't Calm Down
*The Vagus Nerve Reset Plan — 4 Weeks of 5-Minute Exercises to Ease IBS, Bloating & Anxiety*

**Score: 41/50** · **Approach:** Counter-Intuitive / Reframe
**Use if:** the Architect wants to win the click over the established prose competitors on emotional recognition rather than keyword match — this is the strongest "that is exactly my situation" title for the IBS/bloating reader who has already tried diets and probiotics (persona Maya, FACTS.md). It directly reframes the reader's failed prior attempts. Trade-off: lowest searchability (primary keyword sits entirely in the subtitle), so it depends more on cover, ads, and the description to convert. Best as a cover-test challenger against #1, not as the default.
