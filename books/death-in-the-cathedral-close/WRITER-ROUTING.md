## WRITING AGENT BRIEFING

**The three most important things:**

**1. Hook before warmth.**
Chapter 1 opens with wrongness — not named, just felt. The organ playing in the dark. The door ajar. Helen going back to bed and lying awake. The reader should be unsettled before they are charmed. The warmth comes in Chapter 2 onwards. Do not open warmly. Open with dread underneath beauty.

**2. Rosalind must be liked.**
She appears from Chapter 4 onwards. She is warm, competent, genuinely devoted to the cathedral and its music. She is Edmund's closest colleague. She has tea with Helen. She cries at the memorial service (or appears to). The reader must like her — genuinely, not superficially — because the reveal in Chapter 90 only works if the reader feels something complicated at that moment. Not betrayal. Something closer to grief.

**3. Every chapter earns its place.**
This is the Osman complaint fixed by design. Every chapter must either: plant a clue, pay off a clue, advance the investigation, or reveal something about a character that changes how the investigation is read. No atmosphere chapters that go nowhere. The warmth must be embedded in chapters that are doing plot work simultaneously.

**Style targets:**
- Tana French: the opening dread, the slow-burn atmospheric wrongness
- Richard Osman: the warmth, the ensemble life, the community that is worth caring about
- Agatha Christie: the plotting discipline, the clue architecture, the chapter-by-chapter economy

**Voice:**
- Helen POV: short declarative sentences, wry understatement, no performed emotion
- Edmund POV: longer, more circuitous sentences, occasional dry humour, institutional knowledge
- Chapters alternate cleanly between the two — reader always knows immediately whose chapter this is

**The pacing rule:**
A significant event every 2–3 chapters. Not a clue — an event. Something that changes the shape of the investigation or the reader's understanding of a character. In order: body found (Ch 2), Helen's assessment (Ch 3), DS Claire formal involvement (Ch 6), murder confirmed (Ch 9), medication found in archive (Ch 9), midpoint reversal re: batch number (Ch 20), Webb arrested (Ch 26), Webb exculpated + Rosalind identified (Ch 28), Rosalind arrested (Ch 32), denouement (Ch 33–35), series thread (Ch 39–40).

## QUALITY GATE (before submitting each chapter)

1. **Fair-play test:** Would a careful reader rereading this chapter after the reveal find at least one piece of evidence that makes sense in retrospect?
2. **Warmth test:** Is the community worth inhabiting in this chapter? Would a reader want to stay?
3. **Forward momentum test:** Does the chapter end with a question the reader must have answered?
4. **Voice test:** Can you tell from the first sentence whether this is Helen's chapter or Edmund's?
5. **Rosalind test (Chapters 4–89):** If Rosalind appears, is she warm, credible, and likeable — not suspicious in a way that tips the hand?

## SERIES THREAD HANDLING

- The 1887 archive letter is planted in Chapter 39 — Helen's POV
- Do not reference Robert Hartley, Canon Aldous Pemberton, or the east transept matter before Chapter 39
- Rose Hartley appears as background character throughout — present but unremarkable
- The letter discovery in Ch 39 should feel like a whisper, not a revelation — the reader notices it but the chapter does not make a drama of it
- Ch 40 (Edmund POV) makes the connection — "I think there's something in the archive"
- Do not resolve or explain the historical thread — leave it precisely as open as a whisper

## THE DENOUEMENT — CHAPTER 90

Three-beat structure. Non-negotiable.

Three chapters. One beat each.

**Ch 33 — Assembly (Helen's chapter):**
Walk through the clues in order. Each one lands as a small confirmation. The reader thinks: "Yes — I saw that and didn't understand it." This is the reader's reward for paying attention.

**Ch 34 — Killer's Truth (Edmund's chapter, Rosalind speaks):**
Rosalind explains — not excuses. The care home fees. The small withdrawals. Marcus's discovery. The afternoon she made the decision. She does not perform remorse. She is simply honest, possibly for the first time in twelve years. Edmund and Helen must feel something complicated here.

**Ch 35 — The Cost (Helen's chapter):**
The arrest is formal. Rosalind goes quietly — she picks up her pale blue cardigan and puts it on. Edmund watches her leave No. 4 for the last time. The cost: the close will never be entirely the same. A person who devoted thirty years to this place did something unforgivable in it.

Ch 36 (Edmund) closes the denouement arc: Morning Prayer at 7am. The cathedral continues. His institutional loyalty finally resolved — he chose truth, the institution survived it.
