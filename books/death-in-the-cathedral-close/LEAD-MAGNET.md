# Who's Who in Wychford Close
## A Companion Guide for Readers of Death in the Cathedral Close
### The Cathedral Close Mysteries, Book 1 | S. A. Ibrahim

---

*The cathedral close contains forty people living inside medieval walls. Some of them are hiding something. Here is everyone you need to keep track of — and what each one has to lose.*

---

## THE INVESTIGATORS

**Dr Helen Marsh** — No. 7 the Close
Seventy years old. Retired forensic pathologist, thirty-five years in the coroner's office. Now an archive volunteer — which is to say, she has the run of the cathedral's records going back to 1347, and a clinician's eye she cannot switch off. Widowed. One son (Julian) from whom she has been estranged for two years. Always wears a single strand of pearls — a habit from decades of court testimony. Does not say "I think." She says "I note."

**Canon Edmund Hale** — No. 2 the Close
Sixty-eight. Cathedral lawyer: ecclesiastical disputes, Chapter business, property law. Twenty-two years at Wychford. Carries a battered brown leather notebook everywhere. Has a quiet, genuine faith that he does not perform. His dry humour emerges under pressure. Never married. Uses "I wonder if..." when what he means is "I am fairly certain."

---

## THE VICTIM

**Marcus Vayne** — Organist and Director of Music
Fifty-eight. Twenty-five years at Wychford. Brilliant musician. Difficult colleague — three formal complaints in his personnel file, all resolved without action. Had a heart condition: daily digoxin, beta-blockers, statins. Kept his medication in his desk drawer. Kept a locked box in his organ bench (love letters, unrelated to his death). Was found at the foot of the north transept stair at 6:50am.

## THE COMMUNITY

**Canon Rosalind Ferris** — No. 4 the Close
Sixty-two. Precentor — responsible for all cathedral music and worship. Thirty years at Wychford. Edmund's closest colleague. Helen's occasional tea companion. Warm, perpetually competent, devoted to the cathedral. Owns a pale blue cashmere cardigan she wears often. Has sole control of the music fund with no second signatory — against the cathedral's own financial policy.

**Very Revd Patricia Knowles** — The Deanery
Sixty-four. The Dean. First female Dean of Wychford — still a political fact in the Chapter after eight years. In confidential negotiations to sell a cathedral property to fund structural repairs, against Chapter rules. Motivated by genuine necessity. Not hiding a murder; hiding a governance failure.

**Canon Alistair Webb**
Fifty-five. Pastoral canon. Competed for the Director of Music position fifteen years ago. Lost to Marcus. Has resented him since, quietly and consistently. Married (Anne). Also having an affair with a married choir soprano. What he is concealing is the affair, not a murder. But a man who lies about one thing is a man who lies, and DS Marsh knows this.

**DS Claire Marsh** — Wychford CID
Forty-two. Helen's former junior at the coroner's office — Helen mentored her when she was a trainee pathologist before she switched to police work. Short, dark hair. No-nonsense. Respects Helen enormously and cannot always protect her. Uses first names only when the police frame falls away.

**Thomas Oakes** — Night Verger
Fifty-eight. Closes the cathedral at 11pm and does the rounds of the close. Found the body during Morning Prayer setup. Saw someone leaving the organ loft at 11pm on the night of the murder: pale blue cardigan, women's cut, assumed it was Rosalind. Closed the stair door before realising what he was looking at.

**Margaret Holt** — Chapter Clerk
Seventy-five. Institutional memory of the close. Forty years in the cathedral's service. Devoted to Edmund. Brings homemade shortbread to Chapter meetings. Her value to the investigation is that she remembers everything from 1987 onwards, and a surprising amount from before.

**Julian Marsh** — Bristol (not a resident)
Thirty-five. Helen's son. Estranged for two years — his choice. Works in data analytics. Has a connection to Marcus that Helen does not know about: Marcus loaned him twelve thousand pounds nine years ago, and has recently been demanding repayment with interest. Was seen arguing with Marcus in the close the afternoon before the death.

**Rose Hartley** — Song School Administrator
Thirty-eight. Administrative role in the song school. Descended from the Hartley family — a name that appears in the cathedral archives under circumstances not yet fully understood. Does not know the significance of her own family's connection to Wychford.

## KEY QUESTIONS TO HOLD WHILE READING

1. The organ loft is accessible only via one stair. One door. One key. Who had access on the night?
2. Marcus had a heart condition. His medication was in his desk drawer. After the death, the drawer was empty.
3. Thomas Oakes saw a pale blue cardigan leaving the organ loft at 11pm. He assumed it was Rosalind. Why would he assume that?
4. Marcus had written a letter to the Bishop. The letter never arrived. What happened to it?
5. Julian Marsh was in Wychford the afternoon before his mother found the body. His mother did not know he was coming.
