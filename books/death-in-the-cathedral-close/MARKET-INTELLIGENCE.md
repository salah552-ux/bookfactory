## ━━━ SECTION 3: DEEP COMPETITOR AUTOPSY ━━━

### AUTOPSY 1: The Thursday Murder Club (Osman)

**The structural formula:**
- 115 chapters across ~400 pages = average chapter length **~900–1,100 words**
- This is television pacing — each chapter is a scene, not a long sequence
- Every chapter ends on a cliffhanger or unresolved beat
- The investigation runs on two tracks: what the amateurs are doing + what the police are doing
- These tracks converge and diverge, creating ongoing dramatic irony

**Character formula:**
- 4 protagonists with distinct expertise (ex-spy, nurse/diarist, psychiatrist, trade union organiser)
- Each character brings a skill set that makes their involvement credible
- The police detectives (Chris and Donna) are sympathetic, not adversarial
- The amateurs and police develop genuine affection — cooperation replaces competition

**Tone formula:**
- Wit is character-based, never forced
- Mortality, dementia, and loneliness are treated with genuine emotional honesty
- The cosy surface (retirement village, biscuits, committees) contrasts with real darkness underneath
- The humour earns its place because the grief is real

**What works:**
- The ensemble — readers love all four characters
- The warmth — this is comfort reading with emotional weight
- The short chapters — addictive, TV-serial pacing
- The setting — Coopers Chase is a world people want to inhabit

## ━━━ SECTION 6: REPLICATION BLUEPRINT ━━━

*The structural skeleton underneath every successful British cosy mystery.*

```
STRUCTURAL FORMULA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Opening hook type:    A body discovered in an incongruous setting — the violence
                      breaks the cosy surface deliberately. Tonal contract established
                      in chapter 1: this is warm AND dark.

Chapter 1 mandate:   Introduce the setting (specific, loveable community),
                     introduce at least one detective protagonist,
                     deliver the inciting crime. All in under 1,500 words.

Act structure:       TV serial model — not classic 3-act. Think in episodes.
                     Each 3–4 chapters forms a mini-arc with its own question + answer.
                     The overall mystery is the season arc. Each chapter is a scene.

Chapter length:      800–1,200 words. Never more than 1,500. This is the Osman rule.
                     Short = addictive = one-more-chapter at midnight.

POV:                 Maximum 3 POVs. Osman uses 6+ (too many per reader complaints).
                     Recommend: 2 protagonist POVs alternating + occasional antagonist.

Pacing rhythm:       Every chapter must: advance the investigation OR reveal character
                     in a way that changes the investigation. No pure atmosphere chapters.

CHARACTER FORMULA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Protagonist type:    Not "random amateur." Professional with lapsed credentials.
                     Ex-detective, retired pathologist, former barrister — someone
                     with a REASON to be good at this and a REASON to be involved.
                     Age: 60s. The world underestimates them. This is the comedy engine.

Partner type:        Contrasting skill set + contrasting temperament. Creates conflict
                     and comedy through disagreement, not just charm.

Police relationship: NOT "police share everything with strangers." Instead: the
                     protagonist has a PRE-EXISTING RELATIONSHIP with someone in the
                     force. An old colleague. A former student. Their niece. Something
                     that makes the cooperation plausible.

Antagonist:          Present from chapter 2. Appears helpful. Is liked. The reader
                     should feel retrospective guilt for liking them.

PLOT MECHANICS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Body:                Chapter 1 or 2. The victim must be established before death.
                     Give them one vivid, specific quality. Make the murder matter.

Investigation:       Each chapter: one new piece of information, one new question.
                     The investigation must MOVE in every chapter. No treading water.

Midpoint reversal:   ~50% mark. The protagonist's first theory collapses.
                     New direction. The investigation resets with new intelligence.

False accusation:    ~75% mark. Wrong suspect. Almost convincing.
                     Reader may or may not know it's wrong.

Denouement:          3-beat structure: assembly of clues → killer's truth → the cost.
                     The reveal must be emotionally satisfying, not just intellectual.

READER SATISFACTION FORMULA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What readers come for:  The warmth. The wit. The community. The characters they
                        want to spend time with.
What makes them stay:   The mystery actually making sense. Clues they can follow.
                        Suspects they genuinely suspect.
What makes them buy 2:  An unresolved thread in the protagonist's personal life.
                        A relationship question. A series spine that isn't the murder.
                        The murder is solved. The character is still becoming.

NICHE CONVENTIONS (violating these loses readers)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. No graphic violence or gore — the cosy contract is non-negotiable
2. Justice must be served by the end — moral order restored
3. The community must be worth caring about — setting is a character
4. The protagonist must be warm, witty, and competent — not incompetent comedy
5. The mystery must be solvable — fair-play contract honoured
6. At least one chapter must deliver pure delight — the warmth earns the darkness
7. The ending must satisfy emotionally, not just logically
```
