### Category Analysis

**Traditional Detective Mysteries — Is This Correct?**

The book IS a traditional detective mystery structurally — fair-play, puzzle-driven, no supernatural elements, strong amateur investigator. But this is not how the target reader self-identifies or browses. Traditional Detective Mysteries is a legacy category associated with Christie-era hardboiled puzzle fiction. Its audience skews toward readers seeking Agatha Christie, Ngaio Marsh, Josephine Tey — the "golden age" reader. This book fits that audience secondarily.

The primary audience — the reader who buys The Thursday Murder Club, Hamish Macbeth, the Chiltern Hills books — browses Cozy Mystery, not Traditional Detective Mysteries.

**Amateur Sleuths — Is This Correct?**

Yes. Amateur Sleuths is accurate (Helen is an amateur by role, professional by training). The KDP-LISTING.md plan correctly identified this as Category 1 for launch. BSR #24,554 in Amateur Sleuths is weak — the category is competitive and the book is not gaining traction there.

**What Is Missing**

The book is not in:
- Cozy Mystery (Kindle Store > Mystery, Thriller & Suspense > Mystery > Cozy) — **THE primary browse destination for this book's audience**
- British & Irish Mystery & Thrillers — **planned at launch, not live, captures high-loyalty UK series readers**

The OPPORTUNITY-REPORT confirms: every top competitor in the cozy mystery space — Fish and Chip Shop Detectives (BSR 360), Chiltern Hills series, Antique Store Detective — is in the Cozy category. Zero of those competitors appear in Traditional Detective Mysteries.

### The Discoverability Damage

The book is invisible to the Cozy browse audience. When a UK reader opens the Kindle Cozy Mystery category at £0.99 during a Countdown Deal, this book does not appear. It is entirely absent from the browse page that should be its primary discovery channel.

BSR in Traditional Detective Mysteries (#7,668) is actually reasonable for a book with 0 reviews and no marketing — but that audience is the wrong audience. Winning in Traditional Detective Mysteries generates also-bought signals pointing to Christie-era readers, not Thursday Murder Club readers. This feeds the wrong carousel.

### Why Doing This Today Matters

The Countdown Deal is driving the best traffic this book will see until it has reviews and ads. Every buyer who clicks through and purchases during this deal window creates an also-bought signal. If those buyers are reaching the book through Cozy browse, the also-bought chain points to Thursday Murder Club, Chiltern Hills, Antique Store Detective — the right comps. If they arrive through Traditional Detective, the chain points to Agatha Christie and Peter Wimsey — a much smaller modern readership.

Category changes in KDP take 24–72 hours to propagate. If done today (June 2), they will be live by June 4–5 — the mid-deal window when deal traffic typically peaks.
