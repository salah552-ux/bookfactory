## SECTION 1 — WHAT THIS DEAL IS AND WHY IT'S RUNNING NOW

**Situation as of June 1, 2026:**
- Book live: 29 days (May 3 → June 1)
- Current price: £6.99 / $6.99
- Reviews (pipeline record): 0 — but this is likely stale; check Author Central for live count
- KDP Select: enrolled
- Countdown Deal eligible: from June 2 (30-day minimum at full price satisfied)
- Ads gate: closed (requires 10 reviews at 4.0+)

**What a Countdown Deal does to the Amazon algorithm:**

At £0.99, two things happen simultaneously. First, the friction-to-purchase collapses — readers who were browsing and price-hesitant will buy. KU subscribers who browse by price will borrow. Second, the resulting burst of purchases within a compressed window looks to the algorithm exactly like what it is: a velocity event. The A9/A10 ranking system scores sales velocity in the last 24–48 hours more heavily than total lifetime sales. A week of elevated purchases re-evaluates the book's rank, re-exposes it to new audiences through "Also Bought" and "Also Viewed" carousels, and — if reviews land during or after the deal — can push the book toward the ad gate threshold.

**This deal was always part of the architecture.** The marketing-agent specification (Section 3.3) plans a Countdown Deal at Day 45–60. This book is at Day 30 — the earliest legal window. Running it now is not an act of desperation; it is running the planned second push at the first available moment, which makes strategic sense given that the book has had no promotional activity since launch.

**What this deal cannot do:** it cannot substitute for reviews. At 0 reviews, new readers arrive at a listing with no social proof. The deal will generate sales; whether those sales convert to reviews depends on the in-book CTA. Every review that lands during or after this week is worth more than the sale that produced it — it moves the book toward the ad gate.

## SECTION 2 — THE ALGORITHM MECHANICS BEHIND EACH DAY'S ACTIONS

Before the day-by-day plan, understand the mechanism each action is feeding:

**External traffic signal (Section 0.13 of marketing-agent):** Any click arriving at the Amazon product page from outside Amazon — social post, Reddit comment, email link — is recorded by Amazon Attribution. The click signals "this book has audience interest beyond the Amazon ecosystem" and is rewarded with amplified organic placement. Even clicks that do not convert to sales carry this signal. Therefore every social post during deal week must link directly to the Amazon product page URL, not to a landing page or author website.

**UK canonical URL:** `https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B0GZD1S8HF`
**US canonical URL:** `https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GZD1S8HF`

Post both URLs in any social content that is not UK-specific. The US attribution signal feeds back into the global also-bought graph, which influences UK recommendations.

**KU borrow pool:** At £0.99, KU subscribers who might not have noticed the book will borrow it. A KU borrow generates rank signal equivalently to a paid purchase. KU subscribers are a high-volume, low-friction audience — the deal price is irrelevant to them (they pay a flat monthly fee) but the discounted price makes the book more visible in KU "New and Noteworthy" and deal-filtered browse. KU borrows also seed the "Also Read" carousel — every borrow by someone who has read comparable titles (Osman, Ross, Templeton) seeds the co-reading connection that puts this book under those titles permanently.

**Review acceleration window:** Deal buyers are new readers. New readers finish the book within days of buying. The in-book review CTA in the back matter is the only mechanism to convert those reads into reviews. Do not chase reviews on social during the deal — Amazon's systems flag coordinated review requests. Let the back matter do the work.

**The Hot New Releases hourly mechanic (Section 0.12):** Even within a single deal day, purchases concentrated in a 1–3 hour window briefly rank the book as a Hot New Release in its subcategory (Amateur Sleuth, British & Irish Mystery). That ranking generates a burst of impressions, which drives more clicks. The first day of the deal, if any social post is going out, send it between 8am and 10am BST — this is the UK Kindle browsing peak (commute + morning reading window, based on observed UK publishing community patterns — labelled as judgment, not published data) and concentrates the daily velocity into a shorter window for the Hot New Releases signal.

## SECTION 4 — WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE (AND WHAT IT DOES NOT)

**Success is not:** a specific BSR number or a specific unit count. No invented targets — there is no sourced BSR-to-sales data for this specific niche in the pipeline.

**Success is:** the deal generating meaningful velocity compared to the pre-deal baseline. If BSR improves (lower number) during the deal and holds at a better level for even 1–2 weeks post-deal, the algorithmic objective has been achieved. The algorithm has seen a second trust event from this book and re-evaluated it upward.

**The review outcome matters more than the sales outcome.** Ten reviews at 4.0+ opens the ad gate — the mechanism that allows the book to be profitably advertised. Every sale from the deal that converts to a review is worth more than the £0.69 royalty from the sale itself. The deal is as much a review-generation event as a sales event.

**If the deal produces no reviews:** that is data, not failure. It tells you the back matter CTA is not converting buyers to reviewers. That is a fixable problem — the review-bait-optimizer can be re-invoked, or the review request language can be refined. Fix the conversion problem before spending on ads.
