## BACK MATTER CTA SEQUENCE — RATIONALE

The back matter sequence is structured in four beats:

### Beat 1 — World note (grounding, not commercial)
The Wychford note comes first. It does two things: it gently closes the fictional world (the reader is still inside it), and it establishes a tone of warmth and wit that matches the book. It signals: what follows is from the same voice as the story.

### Beat 2 — Review ask (direct, specific, human)
The review CTA immediately follows the world note. Positioning rationale:

- The reader is warm. They just finished the book. They are in the best possible emotional state to act.
- The ask is framed as reader-to-reader, not author-to-reader. "Readers find books because other readers say something about them." This is true and non-manipulative.
- The CTA does not ask for five stars. It asks for a sentence or two. Lower commitment = higher conversion.
- "You don't need to summarise the plot. You can just say what you felt." This is the instruction the reader needs — most non-reviewers don't know how short a review can be.

### Beat 3 — Series hook (Book 2 tease)
The series thread is introduced after the review ask, not before. Positioning rationale: if the series hook appears before the review ask, readers who are excited about Book 2 will click through to find it and never see the CTA. Place the hook after — they have already seen the ask.

The Book 2 tease is written at the same emotional temperature as the book's jacket copy: short, specific, no genre cliches. "Someone does not want the east transept opened" is the hook. It does not over-explain.

The mailing list / author website link follows the Book 2 tease — capturing readers who want to know when it's available. This is the secondary conversion (email capture) after the primary (review).

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- Do not ask for five stars. It feels transactional.
- Do not write "If you enjoyed this book, please consider leaving a review." The word "consider" signals the author doesn't expect it.
- Do not put the review ask after the series hook. The series hook redirects attention.
- Do not include a lengthy author bio before the review ask. The reader doesn't know the author well enough to care yet.
- Do not thank the reader before the review ask. Thanking-in-advance feels presumptuous and softens the ask.
