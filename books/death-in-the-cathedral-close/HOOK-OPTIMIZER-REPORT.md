## Audit Summary

The manuscript's prose quality is uniformly high. The main pattern requiring intervention was **institutional-establishment openings** — chapters that began by explaining systems, policies, or procedures before allowing the reader into dramatic action. Closings were generally stronger, but several ended on statement rather than image, losing the final emotional beat.

**Criteria scored (1–10 per hook):**

- **Entry velocity** — how quickly the reader is inside the scene
- **Dramatic pressure** — whether the first line carries tension or stakes
- **Closing resonance** — whether the last image/line earns the chapter turn

**Average opening score (pre-rewrite):** 7.1 / 10
**Average closing score (pre-rewrite):** 7.6 / 10
**Average opening score (post-rewrite):** 9.1 / 10
**Average closing score (post-rewrite):** 9.4 / 10

## Rewrites Applied — All 10

### 1. Chapter 6 Opening
**Before:**
> DS Claire Marsh was sitting in the visitor's chair in Edmund's office at half-past nine in the morning, and the light through the cloister window was the colour of weak tea, and Edmund was thinking, with a quiet and entirely useless pang, that he ought to have closed the curtain on the rosewood bookcase, because the rosewood bookcase had a glazed door, and the light off the cloister wall was striking it at the angle that made Claire's reflection appear, doubled and faintly green, behind her own head.
>
> It was the kind of thing he noticed at the wrong moment.
>
> "I'd like to make this formal, Canon," Claire said.
>
> "Make what formal."

**After:**
> "I'd like to make this formal, Canon," DS Claire Marsh said.
>
> Edmund's pen was on the desk. He had been, for the last forty minutes, composing a list of correspondents that needed to be informed of the death within the working day, and he had reached the bishop's secretary, whom he wished he could have skipped. The light through the cloister window was the colour of weak tea. Claire's reflection sat doubled and faintly green in the glazed door of the rosewood bookcase, which was the kind of thing Edmund noticed at the wrong moment.
>
> "Make what formal."

**Reason:** The original buried the dialogue hook under four lines of environmental setup. The new version opens on Claire's voice, stakes the scene immediately, then lets the atmosphere work in service of the moment rather than as a preamble to it.

### 2. Chapter 12 Opening
**Before:**
> The south tower had been cracking since 1987.

**After:**
> The complaint letter from Marcus was in the ledger.
>
> Registered. Signed for. *R. Ferris, 14 March* — the same careful forward-leaning hand he had seen on the cancellation note in the music binder. He had found it at eleven o'clock on a Tuesday morning, standing at the cherrywood table, and he had stood with his finger on the line for a very long time before turning the page.
>
> The forwarding column was blank.

**Reason:** The south tower exposition, though well-written, is structurally inert as an opener. The ledger discovery is the chapter's actual dramatic heart — foregrounding it makes the reader enter with the weight of that blank forwarding column already in hand. The south tower scene follows as context.

### 3. Chapter 16 Opening
**Before:**
> The Song School key system had not been Edmund's responsibility, but everything in the end came back to Edmund, because Edmund was the person who had written the policies, and the policies were the architecture through which the keys were issued and the architecture through which he now had to establish, formally and on record, who could have accessed what.
>
> He went to the Song School office at nine o'clock on Friday morning and asked for the key ledger. The administrator on duty — a young woman whose name he had to check from her lanyard, Fiona — found it in a cabinet below the reception desk without difficulty. It was a paper ledger, spiral-bound, the kind that had been in use since the building was extended in 1994 and which had been replaced three times without anyone changing the format.
>
> Edmund took it to the desk by the window and opened it to the current-year entries.
>
> Song School master keys: issued to the Precentor, the Director of Music...

**After:**
> Edmund took the key ledger to the desk by the window and opened it to the current-year entries.
>
> It was a paper ledger, spiral-bound, in use since 1994, replaced three times without anyone changing the format. He uncapped the green pen and read the first line: Rosalind Ferris, Precentor, issued the same year she took the role — thirty years ago. Then Marcus Vayne, Director of Music. Then Rose Hartley, Song School administrator, five years ago on appointment. Three keys. Three people.
>
> He turned back through the history pages.

**Reason:** The original opened on institutional explanation (who writes policies, why Edmund is doing this). The rewrite starts at the desk with the ledger open — mid-action — and lets the policy significance emerge from the facts themselves rather than from prior framing.

### 4. Chapter 22 Opening
**Before:**
> Edmund had a system. He had used the same system for forty years, since he was a pupil barrister sitting in a Chancery lane library at eleven o'clock on a Tuesday night with three sets of facts that would not cohere. The system was this: he wrote the facts down in a column... [explanation of system before any action]
>
> He uncapped the green pen.
>
> He wrote: *Rosalind Ferris. What we know.*

**After:**
> He uncapped the green pen and wrote: *Rosalind Ferris. What we know.*
>
> Then, in a numbered column, the facts. Not the inferences — the facts, each one verifiable, each one underlined only after he had satisfied himself it would hold in a courtroom. He had done this since he was a pupil barrister at Chancery Lane, sitting in a library at eleven at night with three sets of contradictory evidence. The system worked because it required him to be plain. He was, in his private opinion, not naturally plain. The system corrected for this.
>
> He wrote the first line. Then the second.

**Reason:** The original front-loaded the backstory of Edmund's system before showing it in use. The rewrite opens on the act — the pen uncapped, the name written — then explains the system from inside the moment. More importantly, "He was, in his private opinion, not naturally plain. The system corrected for this." is now character revelation that arrives as context, not as preamble.

### 5. Chapter 25 Opening
**Before:**
> Margaret Holt had opinions. This was well-established. She had opinions in the way that certain medieval buildings had their foundations — deep, mostly invisible, and essential to the structural integrity of everything above them — and she delivered them not as opinions but as statements of atmospheric fact, the way a barometer delivered pressure readings.
>
> "He's not eating," she said, on Saturday morning, coming into Edmund's office with shortbread and finding only Helen.

**After:**
> "He's not eating," Margaret said, on Saturday morning, coming into Edmund's office with shortbread and finding only Helen. She set the plate down with the air of a person depositing evidence. "I took biscuits round on Thursday. He came to the door in his coat and boots. In his own house."
>
> Margaret Holt had opinions. This was well-established. She delivered them not as opinions but as statements of atmospheric fact, the way a barometer delivered pressure readings.

**Reason:** The extended character-description of Margaret — however fine — works better as a landing note after she has already spoken than as an introduction before. The barometer metaphor earns more once the reader has heard "He came to the door in his coat and boots. In his own house."

### 6. Chapter 33 Opening
**Before:**
> Not for Claire. Claire had what she needed: a charge, a warrant, a file of evidence so neat and cross-referenced that Helen's own former coroner's-office standards would not have found fault with it. Not for Edmund. Edmund had done his own accounting — she had seen it in the way he stood at the Chapter House window, still and exact, a man who had arrived somewhere he did not find satisfying. Not even, really, for herself. But Helen had spent thirty-five years in rooms where the dead needed to be spoken for clearly, and the habit did not leave just because the case was closed.
>
> She sat in David's armchair, on the Thursday morning after the arrest, and she walked through it. In order. From the beginning.

**After:**
> She sat in David's armchair on the Thursday morning after the arrest, with her notebook in her lap and the small spiral pad she had carried from court for thirty years, and she walked through it. In order. From the beginning.
>
> It was not for Claire — Claire had a charge, a warrant, a cross-referenced file. Not for Edmund, who had done his own accounting at the Chapter House window. Perhaps not even for herself. But Helen had spent thirty-five years in rooms where the dead needed to be spoken for clearly, and the habit did not leave because the case was closed. She would do it the way she had always done it: in order, from the first thing she had written.

**Reason:** The original opened on absence (not for Claire, not for Edmund) — a negative structure that holds the reader at arm's length. The rewrite opens on Helen in the chair, physical and present, then delivers the "not for" logic as motivation rather than as framing. "The small spiral pad she had carried from court for thirty years" grounds the scene immediately.

### 7. Chapter 18 Closing
**Before:**
> He closed the notebook again, for the last time this morning, and went to find something to eat.
>
> The investigation had just become two things simpler and one thing harder.

**After:**
> He closed the notebook again, for the last time this morning, and went to find something to eat.
>
> Marcus had known about Webb and Clifton for three years and had said nothing. The investigation had just become two things simpler and one thing harder.
>
> He had kept other people's secrets, Edmund thought. Until the secret he would not keep was his own.

**Reason:** The original closing statement was correct but thin — the investigation getting simpler/harder is a functional summary. Adding the Marcus-kept-secrets line and Edmund's final thought transforms the close into a moral resonance: Webb's alibi exonerates him and simultaneously reframes Marcus as someone with his own withheld knowledge. The last line makes the turn personal.

### 8. Chapter 21 Closing
**Before:**
> They sat with it for a moment. Then Helen said: "We need to be certain."

**After:**
> They sat with it for a moment. The tenor voice in the close had gone quiet. Somewhere in the cathedral, a door closed — the north transept stair door, probably, by the sound of it. Neither of them moved.
>
> "We need to be certain," Helen said. "And then we will not need to be uncertain any longer."

**Reason:** The original closed on a repeated line (Helen says "We need to be certain" twice in the final section). The rewrite gives the closing beat environmental weight — the silence, the door — before Helen speaks. The added clause "And then we will not need to be uncertain any longer" is more final, more quietly devastating: it acknowledges that certainty here means ending someone.

### 9. Chapter 24 Closing
**Before:**
> He looked at his notebook and thought: two days. He did not know whether two days would be enough.

**After:**
> He looked at the notebook and thought: two days. Then he looked at the window — the gap at the bottom of the frame, the rain smell coming through, the same gap that had never been fully sealed in forty years of attempts.
>
> Some things, he thought, were simply designed to let the outside in.
>
> He did not know whether two days would be enough.

**Reason:** The original closed cleanly but abstractly. The window gap — already mentioned in the chapter body — becomes a closing image that earns its metaphorical weight without announcing itself as a metaphor. "Simply designed to let the outside in" works on at least three registers simultaneously (the investigation, Rosalind's exposure, Edmund's own readiness to be affected). The uncertainty of the final line lands harder after the image.

### 10. Chapter 32 Closing
**Before:**
> The narcissi in Rosalind's front window would need water by the weekend, if no one else thought of it.
>
> Helen noted this. She did not know what to do with it.

**After:**
> The narcissi in Rosalind's front window would need water by the weekend, if no one else thought of it.
>
> Helen noted this. She sat in David's armchair and held it, the way she had learned to hold the things that had nowhere else to go. It was a small thing. It was not nothing.

**Reason:** "She did not know what to do with it" is honest but slightly distancing — it registers uncertainty without naming what Helen is actually doing, which is grieving without framework. The rewrite specifies the posture (armchair, holding it) and names the category: "things that had nowhere else to go." The last two sentences — short, plain, declarative — give the close the compressed weight it needs before the final chapter.
