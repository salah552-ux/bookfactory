## EMAIL 2 — VALUE
**Subject:** The clue hiding in Chapter 2
**Preview text:** Helen notices it. Most readers don't.
**Send:** 48 hours after sign-up

---

Hi,

In Chapter 2, Thomas Oakes finds Marcus at the foot of the north transept stair.

The detail that matters: *Thomas closed the stair door before he realised what he was looking at.*

He's mentioned it in passing, in the same breath as finding the body. And most readers move past it — because the body is the obvious thing. Thomas is elderly. He closed the door automatically. You barely register it.

But Helen registers it.

The door being closed when she arrives means the door was open. Doors in that part of the cathedral do not stand open on their own. Someone left it open deliberately — or didn't think to close it while leaving in a hurry.

This is the kind of reading the book rewards. Not the obvious question of who, but the quieter question of how the scene came to look the way it does. Every detail Thomas provides, Edmund provides, Helen observes — it was placed there. Nothing is incidental.

The companion guide has a list of five questions worth holding as you read. If you haven't opened it yet, the door question is Number One.

S. A. Ibrahim
