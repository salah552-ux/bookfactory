## 1. MARKET ANALYSIS

### What the Top Covers Are Doing

The British cosy mystery category on Amazon UK in April 2026 divides into three dominant visual camps. The first is the **Osman school**: illustrated or stylised covers featuring an ensemble cast in a recognisable location, with warm mid-tones, hand-drawn or slightly retro typography, and an instinctive approachability. The Thursday Murder Club covers (and their imitators: M.R.G. Davies, Robert Thorogood, the Marlow Murder Club) all operate here — warm cream or sage backgrounds, illustrated community, title in a friendly serif or bouncy display face. The second camp is the **police procedural school**: photography-based or hyper-realistic dark imagery, brooding skies, isolated landscape (LJ Ross Northumberland, Aline Templeton Scottish islands), title in heavy sans-serif or condensed bold. These covers signal genre immediately but read cold. The third and fastest-growing camp in 2026 is **typographic minimalism**: bold, oversized title text dominating 70-80% of the cover, minimal illustration, often a single symbolic object or abstract element, high-contrast palette. This is the Richard Osman Book 5 look and the direction Joffe Books is pushing its KU titles.

Dogs are the single most saturated single image in cozy mystery covers in 2025-2026 (confirmed dominant trend, BookCoversbyMelody analysis). Cats are still present in paranormal cozy. Village streets, bookshop interiors, and café settings are the scenic clichés. Female amateur sleuth silhouettes against a setting are present but declining. Any cover featuring a twee cartoon animal, a teacup, or a quaint village green in pastel will be invisible inside the Amazon results page — those covers are indistinguishable from each other.

### What Is Overused and Therefore Invisible

- Illustrated dogs, prominent and realistic, as the lead visual
- Warm cream/sage/moss palettes with hand-lettered or bouncy serif titles
- Village high street or bookshop interior as the establishing setting
- Female silhouette protagonist, often in a colourful coat, shown from behind
- Faux-vintage border treatments and decorative flourishes suggesting Miss Marple pastiche
- "For fans of Richard Osman" taglines (now genre-noise, not signal)
- Comic-warm illustration style that signals "light" to the point of invisibility on a dark screen

### The Visual Gap This Cover Will Own

The cathedral close as a setting has **zero contemporary occupants** in the cosy mystery category. Zero. This is a proven competitive vacuum. The visual opportunity that follows from this: the architecture of a medieval English cathedral — stone, stained glass, long gothic shadows, the specific geometry of columns and vaulted ceilings, the drama of height and dark — gives this cover a visual territory that is both completely unique in the category and immediately legible as British, serious, atmospheric, and literary. No other cover in the category is doing dark gothic stonework alongside warmth.

The colour gap: the category is swimming in warm creams, sage greens, and dusty rose. Navy and stone grey, used with intelligence, own this space entirely. The cover should feel as though it was published by Faber or Jonathan Cape — not as though it belongs on a spinner rack next to a cat who solves crimes.

The typographic opportunity: the title "Death in the Cathedral Close" has the structural weight of the best Christie titles. "Death in" is Pattern A — the strongest proven pattern in the current market (Death on Skye, Death Comes to Marlow, Death in the Borders). The phrase "Cathedral Close" is visually distinctive and completely novel. The typography should treat it like a herald — not a headline.

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION STATEMENT

**Concept:** A medieval English cathedral interior at the threshold between dark and first light — the specific, charged atmosphere of a place that has absorbed seven centuries of human behaviour — suggesting that something irreversible happened here, that the building knows it, and that two people with the right eyes will find it out.

**Mood and References:**
1. *The Name of the Rose* (film, 1986, dir. Annaud) — gothic stone architecture as moral weight; the light-through-darkness compositional logic; the sense that beauty and violence share the same space without contradiction
2. *Close Quarters* by Michael Gilbert (1947 cover, British Crime Club) — the original cathedral mystery; clean mid-century design, the building as character, muted authority rather than decorative warmth
3. Edward Hopper's late interiors — not the American subject matter but the compositional principle: a single light source in deep shadow, the sense of someone having just left or just arrived, the emotional charge of an empty room

**Colour Palette:**

| Name | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Cathedral Night | `#1C2B3A` | Deep blue-black — the sky above a medieval cathedral at 2am. Commands authority. Differentiates from cream-heavy category competitors. |
| Candlelight Gold | `#C9A84C` | Warm brass/gold — the colour of a single candle in a dark nave. The accent that creates warmth without softness. Used for title text and series branding. |
| Stone | `#8A8070` | Medieval limestone — the colour of cathedral walls in morning light. Used for secondary text and architectural detail. |
| Vellum | `#F0EBE0` | The warm off-white of old manuscript paper. Used for author name and series label. |

**Compositional Approach:**
1. **First**: the architecture — the viewer's eye enters at a point of light (stained glass, a candle, a lamp at the organ gallery) set against deep dark stone
2. **Second**: the title — large, authoritative, in the lower two-thirds, occupying the space the way an inscription occupies a monument
3. **Third**: the series label above, the author name below — framing the title like a heraldic device

**Typography Treatment:**
- Title: a classical serif — Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond, or Baskerville — in a large, confident weight, possibly with slight tracking, in candlelight gold against the dark background. The word "Death" should feel declarative, not decorative.
- Series brand: "The Cathedral Close Mysteries | Book One" — small caps, tracked, in vellum or stone, sitting above the title like an imprint line
- Author name: "S. A. Ibrahim" — in vellum or gold, smaller, below the title, with a pair of thin gold rules flanking it

**What the Cover Is NOT Doing:**
- Not warm, not cosy, not pastel — the warmth is architectural, not decorative
- Not illustrative in the twee cartoon sense — no cute village scenes, no animals, no characters shown
- Not a police procedural — no crime scene tape, no dark thriller sans-serif brutalism
- Not a heritage pastiche — no Miss Marple flourishes, no tweed, no vintage border decorations
- Not photography-based — no stock photo of a person or landscape
- Not genre-confused — should read immediately as British literary mystery, not American cozy

### [BRIEF — MAIN DIRECTION]

**Goal:** In the first second, a browser should feel the quiet before something irreversible — beauty holding danger, a cathedral at the threshold of dark and dawn.

**Mood and references:** The Name of the Rose (1986 film) — gothic stonework as moral weight; Hilary Mantel's Wolf Hall Picador covers — authoritative historical serif on atmospheric darkness; the interior photography of David Leventi (cathedrals and opera houses as living archaeology).

**What the cover must communicate:** A murder has been committed inside a medieval English cathedral close — a closed community of forty people, centuries of secrets, and two clever, elderly protagonists who will not let the truth remain buried. Literary, intelligent, genuinely atmospheric. Not cosy in the twee sense. The warmth is the warmth of stone that has held human life for seven hundred years.

**Creative direction:** The dominant visual is the interior of a gothic cathedral nave, viewed from below and toward the organ loft — vast stone columns rising into shadow, a single source of warm light coming from the direction of the organ gallery above, stained glass catching the pre-dawn blue-grey outside. The composition is vertical and solemn. The title occupies the lower half in large, classical gold serif type — the letters feel carved rather than printed. The palette is deep blue-black stone, candlelight gold, and the specific dusty warmth of old limestone. The image should feel like standing at the foot of something ancient that has just witnessed something human.

### [BRIEF — VARIATION A: Stained Glass]

**Goal:** The browser should feel the specific quality of light through medieval glass — coloured and refracted and slightly wrong for the hour — and know instantly that something happened here that the building saw.

**Mood and references:** The stained glass windows in Daisy Hildyard's *The Second Body* cover (Fitzcarraldo Editions, 2017) — minimal, symbolic, deeply atmospheric; Derek Jarman's colour field work — intense saturated colour against deep shadow; the poster for *Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy* (2011) — composed restraint, authority without brutalism.

**What the cover must communicate:** A murder inside a closed ecclesiastical world. Two elderly amateur investigators with genuine professional credibility. Christie plotting in a contemporary setting that no other British mystery currently occupies.

**Text to include (exact strings):**
- Series brand (small, above title): "The Cathedral Close Mysteries"
- Main title: "Death in the Cathedral Close"
- Author: "S. A. Ibrahim"

**Creative direction:** The dominant visual element is a fragment of medieval stained glass — ruby, sapphire, deep amber — filling one side of the cover, shot through with light at an angle that suggests early morning rather than full day. The other side deepens into the blue-black of gothic stone. The title sits across both zones — the letters passing from lit colour into deep shadow — in a heavy classical serif in candlelight gold. The effect should suggest: the light came back through this window every morning for six hundred years and this morning it came back to find something changed.

**Do NOT include:** A cartoon or vector representation of stained glass; bright primary colours in a naive style; modern church windows; a human figure; any illustrative warmth or coziness; text other than the three listed strings; a plain colour block without texture.

### [BRIEF — VARIATION B: The Organ Loft]

**Goal:** The browser should feel the dread of an empty seat at an instrument that was being played an hour ago in the dark — specific, charged, literary.

**Mood and references:** Stanley Kubrick's *The Shining* compositional logic (dread in a beautiful space, not in an ugly one); the Faber & Faber covers for *Notes from Underground* (spare, cold, monumental); the specific piano-in-an-empty-room atmosphere of Tarkovsky's *Nostalghia* (1983).

**What the cover must communicate:** The organist of Wychford Cathedral is dead. A retired forensic pathologist knew within sixty seconds that he didn't fall. The cathedral close — forty people inside a medieval wall — is hiding the answer. A series that takes mystery seriously.

**Text to include (exact strings):**
- Series brand (small, above title): "The Cathedral Close Mysteries"
- Main title: "Death in the Cathedral Close"
- Author: "S. A. Ibrahim"

**Creative direction:** The view is upward toward an organ loft — the massive pipes visible in deep shadow, the gallery railing just perceptible, the bench empty. A single gallery lamp casts a warm yellow ellipse into the scene; everything beyond it is the blue-black of a cathedral at two in the morning. The composition is vertiginous, the ceiling lost in shadow above, the instrument overwhelming in scale. The title is set in the lower third — classical gold serif, large and deliberate, as though the instrument's weight pressed it down. The mood is: something played here in the dark and then went silent.

**Do NOT include:** A cartoon organ or music-note decoration; a human figure at the organ; overtly horror-style imagery; bright lighting or warm comfortable atmosphere; text other than the three listed strings; any animals or village-scene elements.

## 4. INTERIOR FORMATTING SPEC

### Manuscript Structure (confirmed from files)

The manuscript contains:
- `00-00-copyright.md` — front matter copyright page
- `ch-001.md` through `ch-040.md` — 40 chapters (approximately 84,000 words)
- `99-back-matter.md` — author's note, review CTA, series preview, about author

No phase separator pages are needed. This is a chapter-by-chapter novel structure, not a phase-based non-fiction book. Special interior elements confirmed from manuscript audit: **scene breaks** (the `---` horizontal rule at the end of each chapter before the word count line) and **chapter headings** with both a chapter number and a chapter title.

### For Kindle (eBook — DOCX or HTML submission)

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Body font | EB Garamond or Georgia, 11–12pt equivalent |
| Line spacing | 1.15 |
| First paragraph after chapter heading | No indent |
| Subsequent paragraphs | 0.3" indent, no extra spacing between paragraphs |
| Chapter heading (H1) | Chapter number in small caps + chapter title on next line |
| Scene break | Three centred spaced asterisks: `* * *` |
| Front matter order | Title → Copyright → Dedication (if added) → TOC → Chapters |
| Back matter order | Author's Note → Review CTA → Series Preview → About the Author → Also By |

**Note:** The `---` separator at the end of each chapter in the manuscript files is the end-of-chapter marker and word count metadata. This must be stripped from the production file. The actual scene breaks within chapters (there are none — each chapter is a single unbroken scene) do not require `* * *` markers.

### For Print (Paperback — 5.5 × 8.5" Recommended)

**Trim size:** 5.5 × 8.5" — the British literary fiction and cosy mystery standard. At 2,100 words × 40 chapters = ~84,000 words, this yields approximately 280–310 pages at 11pt EB Garamond with standard margins, which is ideal for the category (not too thin to feel substantial, not too thick to feel intimidating).

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Trim size | 5.5 × 8.5" |
| Inside margin (gutter) | 0.875" |
| Outside margin | 0.5" |
| Top margin | 0.75" |
| Bottom margin | 0.75" |
| Body font | EB Garamond 11pt (print) |
| Line spacing | 13.5pt leading (approximately 1.23×) |
| Header | Chapter title, Garamond small caps, 9pt, outside margin — suppress on chapter opener pages |
| Footer | Page number, centred, Garamond 9pt — suppress on chapter opener pages |
| Running head (recto) | Chapter title |
| Running head (verso) | Book title |

**Spine width calculation (84,000 words ÷ ~280 words/page = ~300 pages):**
300 pages × 0.0025" = **0.75" spine** — well above the 79-page minimum for spine text. Spine text: "Death in the Cathedral Close · S. A. Ibrahim" — vertical, reading bottom to top, in Garamond small caps.

### Special Interior Elements (from Manuscript Audit)

**Chapter Opener Style:**
Each chapter has two levels: a chapter number line (`# CHAPTER ONE`) and a chapter title line (`## The Organ at Two in the Morning`). These must be treated as a composed unit — not two separate headings. See Section 5 for the recommended treatment.

**Scene Breaks:**
No mid-chapter scene breaks are present in the reviewed manuscript. The `---` separator in each file is end-of-chapter metadata (word count/status) — strip entirely in production.

**No Required Callout Boxes:** This is a novel, not non-fiction. No callout boxes, tables, or phase separators are needed.

**Epigraph possibility:** The manuscript does not currently include an epigraph. If one is added by the author before final production, it should be set in Garamond italic, centred, with attribution in small caps 2 lines below, on its own page between the dedication and Chapter 1.

**Drop caps:** Optional but strongly recommended for Chapter 1 only — a 3-line drop cap on "T" ("The first wrong note woke her") signals literary production values at the chapter-one open. Subsequent chapters: no drop cap (they would interrupt the two-POV alternation rhythm that the reader needs to track visually).

## 5. CHAPTER HEADER RECOMMENDATION

**Recommended: Option 2 — Clinical with Warmth, modified for fiction**

Rationale: This is a literary mystery with two alternating POVs (Helen/Edmund). The chapter headers must do two jobs: signal the chapter number clearly (readers tracking progress) and signal the POV shift implicitly (through consistency of position and style). Option 1 (small caps only) is too austere for the warmth this book earns. Option 3 (phase-aware) is for phase-structured non-fiction. Option 2 — large numeral with title — serves both functions.

**Proposed treatment:**

```
[centred, Garamond or Cormorant]

CHAPTER                         ← 9pt tracked small caps, muted (#5a6472 or equivalent)
 
ONE                             ← 48pt, light weight, Cathedral Night navy (#1C2B3A)

The Organ at Two in the Morning ← 20pt italic, Garamond, below a thin 0.5pt rule
                                    (the rule sits between the numeral and the title)
```

The numeral in large, light weight reads as an elegant editorial choice — it echoes the style of the Jonathan Cape and Faber literary mystery tradition, which is exactly where this book positions itself. The italic chapter title in a smaller weight beneath creates hierarchy without competing with the numeral. The thin rule between numeral and title is the series brand's consistent dividing gesture.

**POV indicator (optional — recommended for series clarity):** A very small, tracked label in muted grey — "HELEN" or "EDMUND" — can sit 6pt above the word "CHAPTER" in 7pt all-caps Garamond. This is a navigational aid, not a design feature. It should be barely visible to a reader reading normally and immediately useful to a reader who has put the book down and picked it up again. Confirm with author before implementing.

## 8. COVER COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION SCRIPT

Run this after Nano Banana generation and before presenting any cover to the Architect. No exceptions.

### Step 1 — Resize and Convert (Sharp)

```js
const sharp = require('sharp');
sharp('cover-raw.png')
  .resize(1600, 2560, { fit: 'fill' })
  .jpeg({ quality: 95, chromaSubsampling: '4:4:4' })
  .toFile('exports/final/cover-nano-banana-kdp.jpg');
```

### Step 2 — Verify All 8 Checks

```js
const sharp = require('sharp');
const fs = require('fs');
const f = 'exports/final/cover-nano-banana-kdp.jpg';
sharp(f).metadata().then(m => {
  const size = fs.statSync(f).size;
  const pass = (check, val) => console.log((check ? '✓' : '✗'), val);
  pass(m.format === 'jpeg',                               'Format: ' + m.format);
  pass(m.width === 1600,                                  'Width: ' + m.width);
  pass(m.height === 2560,                                 'Height: ' + m.height);
  pass(m.space === 'srgb',                                'Color space: ' + m.space);
  pass(m.channels === 3,                                  'Channels: ' + m.channels + ' (no alpha)');
  pass(m.hasAlpha === false,                              'Has alpha: ' + m.hasAlpha);
  pass(size < 50 * 1024 * 1024,                          'Size: ' + Math.round(size/1024/1024*100)/100 + 'MB');
  pass(Math.round(m.height/m.width * 10)/10 === 1.6,    'Ratio: ' + Math.round(m.height/m.width*1000)/1000);
});
```

**All 8 must show ✓ before the cover goes to Architect review.**

## 11. SERIES BRANDING SYSTEM — THE CATHEDRAL CLOSE MYSTERIES

This is Book 1 of a planned 3-book series. The series brand must be established in Book 1 and carried forward with precision. Any design decision in Book 1 becomes a series constraint — make it deliberately.

### Series Colour Identity System

Each book gets a **secondary accent colour** layered over the shared palette. The core (Cathedral Night, Vellum, Candlelight Gold, Stone) never changes. The accent shifts per book to make them visually distinguishable on a "Also By" shelf while being instantly recognisable as a family.

| Book | Title | Secondary Accent | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| Book 1 | Death in the Cathedral Close | **No secondary accent** — gold only | First in series; establish the core identity cleanly |
| Book 2 | The Archivist's Secret | **Verdigris** `#4A7B6F` | The archive, old copper, documents — the historical secret surfacing |
| Book 3 | A Requiem for the Dean (tentative) | **Deep Burgundy** `#6B1E3A` | Requiem, culmination, the institutional reckoning |

### Cover Family Rules (apply to all three books)

1. **Shared background**: Cathedral Night `#1C2B3A` as the dominant field — all three covers read as a family from across a bookshop table
2. **Shared title typographic treatment**: large classical serif (Cormorant Garamond or Garamond Premier), Candlelight Gold, occupying the lower 50–60% of the cover
3. **Shared series label**: "The Cathedral Close Mysteries" — same position (top, small caps, Vellum), same size, same font across all three
4. **Shared author treatment**: "S. A. Ibrahim" — same position (bottom, with thin gold rules flanking), same weight and colour across all three
5. **Architectural visual**: each cover features a different element of the cathedral close — Book 1 (the nave/organ loft), Book 2 (the archive/chapter house), Book 3 (the east transept/the historical wound). The setting itself is the visual through-line
6. **No characters shown**: none of the three covers depicts Helen, Edmund, or any other person. The building is the protagonist of the series design

### Spine System (all three books)

- Background: Cathedral Night `#1C2B3A`
- Book title: Garamond small caps, Vellum `#F0EBE0`, reading bottom to top
- Author name: directly below or above the title, Garamond small caps, Candlelight Gold `#C9A84C`
- Series number indicator: optional small arabic numeral (1, 2, 3) at the bottom of the spine in Stone `#8A8070`
- No publisher imprint on spine (standing rule — no exceptions)

### Interior Series Consistency

| Element | Standard (all three books) |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Body font | EB Garamond 11pt (print), Georgia (Kindle fallback) |
| Chapter heading | Large numeral + italic title (see Section 5) |
| Chapter opener | No drop cap except Chapter 1 of Book 1 (series signature) |
| Scene break | Three centred asterisks: `* * *` |
| Running heads | Recto: chapter title · Verso: book title |
| Back matter order | Author's Note → Review CTA → Series Preview → About the Author → Also By |
| Series preview | Always preview the *next* book — 2-paragraph teaser with title |
