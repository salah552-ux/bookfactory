# SERIES PLAN — The Cathedral Close Mysteries
## Created by: novel-writer | 24 April 2026

---

## SERIES SPINE

**The overarching question:** What did Wychford Cathedral do in 1887 — and what would it cost to bury that truth today?

Each book solves its own contemporary murder. But each book also surfaces one more fragment of the cathedral's 1887 secret. The reader accumulates understanding across the three books. By the final book, the historical wrong and the present-day murder are inseparable.

**Reader retention hook:** Every book ends with the contemporary mystery solved — fully satisfying. But the last pages of every book open one new door in the historical thread. The reader cannot leave the series until that door is opened.

**Series protagonists:** Dr Helen Marsh + Canon Edmund Hale — same duo throughout.

**Series tone constant:** Warmth, wit, dread. The cathedral as a character that never fully reveals itself.

---

## BOOK 1 — DEATH IN THE CATHEDRAL CLOSE

**Premise:** The cathedral organist is found dead at the foot of the organ gallery. It looks like a fall. Dr Helen Marsh knows within sixty seconds that it is not.

**Central conflict:** Finding the killer inside a closed community where everyone has something to hide — including Helen's estranged son.

**Resolution:** Canon Rosalind Ferris is unmasked. The murder was an act of panic by a fundamentally decent person who made one dishonest choice twelve years ago and couldn't stop.

**What is resolved:** The murder. Julian's partial reconnection with Helen.

**What remains open:** The 1887 letter found in the archive. A payment "in settlement of the matter of the east transept" to a family called Hartley. One of the Hartleys works in the song school today.

**Series hook:** Edmund: "Helen, I think there's something in the archive." Helen: "I know. I've thought so for some time."

**Standalone rating:** Yes — fully satisfying as a standalone. Series thread is a whisper, not a cliffhanger.

---

## BOOK 2 — THE ARCHIVIST'S SECRET

**Premise:** Cathedral renovation work in the east transept uncovers human remains — skeletal, Victorian-era. Before the archaeologists can examine them, the site supervisor is found dead. Someone does not want the east transept opened.

**How it escalates:** Helen is now fully integrated into the close community. She has a stake. The historical mystery and the contemporary murder are directly connected for the first time. Edmund's institutional loyalty is genuinely tested — the historical wrong was committed by the cathedral, not a person who has since left.

**New element introduced:** A Hartley family descendant — Rose Hartley, 38, who works in the song school — becomes a central figure. She knows more about her family's history with the cathedral than she initially admits.

**What Book 2 reveals of the 1887 secret:** The human remains are identified. They were a stonemason named Robert Hartley. The cathedral paid his family to say he died in an accident. He was killed — covered up to protect the reputation of a then-canon during the cathedral's most politically sensitive period.

**Resolution:** The contemporary murder solved. The 1887 cover-up named — but the canon who committed it has living descendants in positions of cathedral influence. That thread opens.

**Series hook:** Rose Hartley, at the very end: "There's one more thing. My grandmother left a box. I haven't opened it."

**Standalone rating:** Mostly — the contemporary mystery is complete. But readers who read Book 1 will recognise the deepening thread.

---

## BOOK 3 — A REQUIEM FOR THE DEAN

**Premise:** Very Revd Patricia Knowles — the Dean of Wychford — is found dead in the Deanery. She was the one person in the close who knew the full truth about 1887. The box Rose Hartley's grandmother left turns out to contain the original suppressed inquest documents — including a letter signed by a canon whose name is still on the cathedral's roll of honour.

**How it escalates:** Everything converges. The historical crime and the contemporary murder are the same story. The killer is protecting a legacy — an institutional reputation, a family name, a bequest that would be void if the truth emerged.

**New element introduced:** The Bishop becomes involved. The discovery threatens not just Wychford but the wider Church's relationship with its own history. Edmund must choose between his institution and the truth — definitively, irreversibly.

**What Book 3 resolves:** The 1887 murder named, the cover-up chain traced to the present day, the contemporary killer unmasked. Edmund makes his choice — he goes public. The cathedral is shaken. But it survives, as cathedrals do.

**Series arc resolution:**
- The question answered: What happened to Robert Hartley in 1887 — and who in the present was willing to kill to keep it buried.
- The transformation complete: Helen began as a useful outsider; she ends as someone the close cannot imagine without. Edmund began as an institutional loyalist; he ends as a man who chose truth over institution and discovered the institution survived it.
- The world state at end: The cathedral continues. Morning prayer, evensong, the choir, the organ. The east transept has a small plaque now — Robert Hartley, stonemason, 1851–1887. Helen planted it. Edmund said the words.

**Final image (Book 3):** Helen and Edmund at evensong. The choir sings. The coloured light falls. It is the same cathedral. It is not the same cathedral at all.

## CROSS-BOOK CONSISTENCY RULES

These facts must never change or contradict across all three books:

**Characters:**
- Helen Marsh: 70 at Book 1, retired forensic pathologist, archive volunteer, pearls, estranged (then partially reconciled) son Julian
- Edmund Hale: 68 at Book 1, cathedral lawyer, leather notebook, carries quiet faith, growing feelings for Helen (never declared in Book 1, declared in Book 2, accepted in Book 3)
- DS Claire Marsh: 42, Helen's former junior, complex loyalty, promoted to DI between Books 1 and 2
- Margaret Holt: 75, chapter clerk, absolute institutional memory, comic warmth constant
- The cathedral: Wychford Cathedral, medieval, English, fictional city of Wychford (Somerset-adjacent, not named county)

**The close geography:**
- Organ loft accessible only via narrow stone stair from the north transept
- Archive is in the cellar beneath the Chapter House — Helen's key access
- Edmund's office: Chapter House annex, first floor, wood-panelled
- The close has 12 residential properties; No. 4 was Rosalind's (vacant from Book 2)

**Series thread locked facts:**
- Payment made 1887 to Hartley family — "settlement of east transept matter"
- Robert Hartley, stonemason, died 1887 — officially accidental, actually covered up
- The canon responsible: Canon Aldous Pemberton (1840–1901) — his portrait hangs in the Chapter House
- The Hartley family: Rose Hartley (38) is the relevant living descendant; works in the song school from Book 1
- The box: Rose's grandmother's box contains inquest documents + Pemberton's letter. Opened in Book 3.
