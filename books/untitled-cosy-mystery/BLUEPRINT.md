## CREATIVE CONCEPT — LOCKED

**LOGLINE:**
"A retired forensic pathologist and a cathedral lawyer must unmask a killer hiding behind thirty years of devotion — before the wrong man is charged for it and the murderer disappears behind a closed community's silence."

**PREMISE:**
When the cathedral organist Marcus Vayne is found dead at the foot of the organ gallery, the death looks like an accident — a lonely man who lost his footing in the dark. Dr Helen Marsh sees otherwise within sixty seconds. What follows is an investigation inside a medieval English cathedral close, a community of forty people who have lived, worked, and kept each other's secrets for decades.

**THEME:**
The things institutions protect are not always the things worth protecting.

**CORE TENSION:**
Helen and Edmund are investigating their own community — people they know, like, and trust. Every clue they find implicates someone they care about. The closer they get to the truth, the more it will cost them personally.

**THE READER PROMISE:**
A book you cannot put down after chapter three, that makes you love two people by chapter eight, that makes you suspect everyone by chapter twenty, and that makes you feel something complicated in the final pages — not just satisfaction, but grief for what the cathedral close lost when the truth came out.

**THE REVIEW GAP THIS FILLS:**
Two clean POVs (not six). Every chapter advances the mystery. Professional credibility for the investigation. The warmth of Osman with the plotting discipline of Christie.

## SERIES / STANDALONE DECISION

**DECISION: SERIES — 3 books planned**

- Series title: **The Cathedral Close Mysteries**
- Arc type: Character series — same protagonists, standalone cases per book, overarching cathedral secret threading through all three
- Book 1 ending: Fully resolved murder. Personal thread left open (Helen's estranged son Julian; Edmund's growing feelings for Helen — unspoken).
- The series spine: The cathedral itself is hiding something from its own history — a centuries-old wrong that surfaces one fragment at a time across the three books.

### THE KILLER — CANON ROSALIND FERRIS, 62

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Role | Precentor — responsible for all cathedral music and worship |
| Motive | Marcus discovered she had been quietly embezzling from the music fund for 12 years — small amounts used to support her mother in a care home. He was going to expose her at the next Chapter meeting. |

## WORLD ARCHITECTURE

**Primary location:** Wychford Cathedral Close — a medieval English cathedral and its surrounding community of clergy housing, the Chapter House, the archive, the song school, and the deanery. Enclosed by a medieval wall. Population: approximately forty permanent residents.

**Time period:** Present day, April.

**Five sensory anchors:**
1. Cold stone and candle wax — the cathedral smell, specific and inescapable
2. The organ in rehearsal — muffled through the close walls, always slightly eerie at a distance
3. The damp chill of the archive cellar where Helen works
4. The particular quality of light through medieval stained glass — coloured and specific, never the same twice
5. The bell marking each hour — the close's heartbeat

**Key locations:**
- The Organ Loft — where Marcus died. 18 feet above the nave floor, accessible only by a narrow stone stair from the north transept.
- The Archive — Helen's domain. Cellar beneath the Chapter House. Cold, damp, centuries of secrets.
- Edmund's Office — Chapter House annex, first floor. Wood-panelled. View over the cloister garth.
- No. 4, the Close — Rosalind's house. Neat, musical, piano visible through the front window.
- The Song School — ground floor, south side. CCTV in the corridor outside.
- The Cathedral — the space where everything converges.

### ACT I — THE BODY IN THE LOFT (Chapters 1–10)

**Goal:** World, death, protagonists, suspects 1–3. Hook by end of Ch 1. Reader loves Helen and Edmund by Ch 8.
**Act I lock:** Ch 6 — DS Claire formally involves Helen and Edmund. They are committed.

---

**Ch 1 | HELEN | "The Organ at Two in the Morning"**
Helen wakes at 2am. Hears organ music — wrong-sounding, stopping mid-phrase. The close at night. She checks the loft door: ajar. She locks her own door and goes back to bed. The organ loft light was off. The music had been playing in the dark.
- DREAD-FIRST: wrongness felt before named. Helen is wrong to go back to bed. The reader knows it.
- No murder yet. No body. Pure atmosphere and unease.

**Ch 2 | EDMUND | "Morning Prayer"**
Thomas Oakes finds Marcus at 7am. Edmund calls 999. Calls Helen — "Something has happened." The close's reaction catalogued: Rosalind collapses (or performs collapse — Edmund notes, regrets noting). Webb goes very quiet. The Dean's first question: "Will this affect Easter services?"
- Marcus established through others' grief — brilliant, difficult, 25 years, loved and resented.
- First full picture of the close community.

**Ch 3 | HELEN | "Sixty Seconds"**
Helen examines the scene before police process it. Sixty seconds of pathologist's eye. "He didn't fall, Edmund. Look at his hands." Body position wrong. Temperature wrong — dead longer than position suggests.
- **CLUE 1 planted:** The coffee mug on the organ console, oddly clean. Buried in Helen's list of observations between two other details.
- **CLUE 5 setup:** Thomas mentions, as background colour, that he saw a figure in a pale blue cardigan leaving the loft at 11pm. He assumed it was Rosalind — she checks on rehearsals sometimes.
- DS Claire Marsh arrives. Sees Helen. "Oh God."

**Ch 4 | EDMUND | "Medication"**
Edmund in Marcus's office with Claire. The desk: medication bottles present — beta-blockers, statins. One gap.
- **CLUE 2 planted:** "His digoxin seems to be missing." Edmund mentions it to Claire as an afterthought. She notes it. Neither acts on it immediately.
- Suspects introduced: Webb's visible hostility to news of death. Julian Marsh arrives at the close — unannounced, unexpected, connected to Marcus somehow.
- Rosalind appears: warm, devastated, immediately offers to handle the music schedule.

**Ch 5 | HELEN | "A Condolence Visit"**
Rosalind calls on Helen with shortbread and genuine warmth. They talk about Marcus, the close, the shock.
- **CLUE 3 planted:** Rosalind says she stopped by the organ room last night "to remind Marcus about tomorrow's rehearsal." Helen checks her cathedral diary that evening: Thursday choir practice was cancelled three weeks ago.
- Helen registers the discrepancy. Says nothing. Files it.
- Julian's connection to Marcus unexplained — he avoids Helen's questions.
- Rosalind must read as warm, credible, and devastated. The reader must like her after this chapter.

**Ch 6 | EDMUND | "The Policy"**
DS Claire formally opens inquiry. Edmund appointed cathedral liaison — he coordinates access, she receives information. The arrangement made.
- Dean's secret property negotiation surfaces as rumour — red herring planted.
- **CLUE 4 planted:** Edmund auditing financial records discovers the music fund has no second signatory. Against cathedral financial policy — policy Edmund himself wrote twelve years ago and let lapse. His self-reproach is the scene's focus. Rosalind's name is present but not pointed at.
- Act I lock: Helen and Edmund are committed.

**Ch 7 | HELEN | "Cardigan"**
Helen interviews Thomas Oakes properly.
- **CLUE 5 fully planted:** Pale blue cashmere cardigan. 11pm. Woman's, he thinks. He assumed Rosalind — she sometimes checks on Marcus's late rehearsals — and didn't treat it as significant.
- Julian's loan from Marcus partially revealed — he was seen arguing with Marcus in the close the day before the death.
- Edmund joins — connects cardigan to Rosalind immediately, then immediately dismisses his own connection. "She owns one. So does half the close."

**Ch 8 | EDMUND | "The Audit"**
Edmund formally audits the music fund.
- **CLUE 4 deepened:** Twelve years of small withdrawals — individually trivial, cumulatively significant. Edmund calls it a discrepancy. He does not yet name who is responsible.
- Webb confronted informally — evasive, hostile, genuinely frightened. Red herring deepened.
- Rosalind brings Edmund tea while he works. Asks gentle questions about how the investigation is going. Her hands are steady.

**Ch 9 | HELEN | "Murder"**
Post-mortem preliminary: digoxin toxicity at level inconsistent with prescribed dose. Murder confirmed. Inquiry formally opens.
- **CLUE 2 partial payoff:** Marcus's missing digoxin bottle found in the archive storeroom — not his office, not his home. Helen finds it. Her hands are shaking.
- Rosalind at the memorial service. She cries. The reader watches her cry.
- The close changes when the word murder enters it.

**Ch 10 | EDMUND | "No Rehearsal"**
Edmund checks the cathedral schedule himself.
- **CLUE 3 paid off:** No Thursday choir practice scheduled for that week. Cancelled three weeks prior. Rosalind's stated reason for visiting Marcus was false.
- **CLUE 1 paid off:** Lab results — digoxin traces in the mug residue. The mug was deliberately wiped.
- **CLUE 4 partial payoff:** Music fund: twelve years of small withdrawals confirmed by full audit.
- Both Helen and Edmund now hold the same unspoken knowledge. Neither names it.
- Last line: "He said nothing to Helen. He needed to be certain first."

### ACT II — THE INVESTIGATION (Chapters 11–30)

**Goal:** Investigation deepens. All suspects active. Midpoint reversal at Ch 20 reframes access question. False accusation at Ch 26. Exculpation at Ch 28.

---

**Ch 11 | HELEN | "The Loan"**
Julian's full account: the £12,000 loan, the demand for repayment, the argument in the close.
- **Critical timeline fact:** Julian saw Marcus leaving Rosalind's house at 9:30pm the night of the murder. He didn't mention it — didn't know it mattered.
- Helen cannot yet take this to Claire. She doesn't know what it means in full.
- Last line: "Whatever happened between 9:30 and 7am had happened in the organ loft."

**Ch 12 | EDMUND | "The Letter"**
Dean's secret property negotiation explained — south tower structural damage, genuine necessity. Red herring 2 closed.
- Marcus's formal complaint letter to the Bishop revealed: about financial irregularities in the cathedral.
- Rosalind, as Precentor, had signed for all Chapter correspondence. The letter never reached the Bishop.
- Last line: "The letter had never reached the Bishop. Rosalind had signed for it herself."

**Ch 13 | HELEN | "Webb"**
Webb formally questioned — evasive, hostile, frightened. Red herring at maximum intensity.
- Rosalind volunteers to coordinate the cathedral's response to the investigation. Everyone grateful.
- Helen watches both of them. She knows which one is guilty. She cannot prove it.
- Claire: "Webb is our most likely suspect." Helen: "Probably." Nothing else.

**Ch 14 | EDMUND | "Love Letters"**
The locked box in Marcus's organ bench found by Claire's team. Edmund present when it's opened.
- Contents: love letters from a woman named Catherine, dead nineteen years. Deeply personal. Murder-irrelevant.
- Red herring 4 introduced and closed in the same chapter. Edmund replaces the letters. Marcus becomes human.
- Margaret Holt: institutional memory deployed as warmth and comedy. The investigation appears stuck.

**Ch 15 | HELEN | "The Second Bottle"**
Helen traces the digoxin batch number in the archive bottle. It does not match Marcus's prescription.
- A second bottle. Obtained separately. Off-prescription. Cash payment.
- Someone obtained digoxin specifically in order to kill Marcus Vayne.
- Access question: who had a Song School key to access the archive corridor?

**Ch 16 | EDMUND | "Keys"**
Edmund establishes the Song School key system clearly on record.
- Only three people hold Song School keys: Rosalind (Precentor), Marcus (Director of Music — now dead), Rose Hartley (song school administrator).
- Webb has never held a Song School key — this is established as a documented fact.
- Rosalind chairs the Chapter meeting with effortless competence. Edmund watches her.
- Last line: "She was very good at this."

**Ch 17 | HELEN | "Care Home"**
Helen traces the music fund withdrawals to their destination.
- Rosalind's mother. Dementia. Private care home. Twelve years of fees, paid in small instalments from the music fund. The mother died eighteen months ago. The withdrawals stopped.
- Marcus discovered this three months before the story begins. He gave Rosalind the chance to repay quietly. Then he refused quiet repayment and demanded disclosure to the Chapter.
- Motive fully assembled. Last line: "She had been paying for her mother's care. With money that was not hers. And Marcus had found out."

**Ch 18 | EDMUND | "The Affair"**
Sarah Clifton officially confirms Webb's alibi. The affair is now known.
- Red herring 1 fully closed. Webb's evasion, fear, and inconsistency — all explained by a secret entirely unrelated to the murder.
- Claire is under pressure from her superiors to close the case. The inquiry has been open for weeks.
- The investigation appears to narrow toward Webb — wrongly. Claire has no reason to look elsewhere.

**Ch 19 | HELEN | "Tea"**
Helen calls on Rosalind. They spend an afternoon together. Helen is testing her own certainty.
- Rosalind: warm, thoughtful, appalled by the Webb rumours. "An innocent person destroyed is a second tragedy."
- Helen walks home. Thinks about what Rosalind said for a long time.
- Last line: quietly devastating. Helen knows. And she still doubts herself. That doubt is a form of decency.

**Ch 20 | EDMUND | "MIDPOINT — The Batch Number"**
The full toxicology lab report arrives.
- The digoxin batch number in the archive storeroom matches Marcus's prescription bottle — which is also in the storeroom.
- Which means: whoever put the bottle in the archive also had Marcus's original medication. The killer took his prescription, obtained a lethal second dose, used it, then planted the original to suggest he'd left it there himself.
- Song School corridor CCTV requested. Edmund calls Helen: "I need you to see something."
- Everything the investigation assumed about access is now wrong.

**Ch 21 | HELEN | "The Footage"**
CCTV footage from Song School corridor: a figure entering the archive storeroom at 10:15pm, night of the murder. The cardigan is pale. The image is not sharp enough to identify a face.
- Enhancement requested. Will take days.
- Helen and Edmund name it to each other. Finally, in words.
- Last line: "They sat with it for a moment. Then Helen said: 'We need to be certain.'"

**Ch 22 | EDMUND | "Building"**
Helen and Edmund assemble the case privately — they cannot go to Claire yet.
- **CLUE 4 full payoff:** Cash withdrawal from music fund matches to the day and amount the private pharmacist would have charged for an off-prescription digoxin dispensation.
- Full timeline reconstructed: Rosalind at organ room 9:30pm (Julian's sighting) → Marcus unconscious by 10pm → pushed from gallery 10:30pm → Thomas sees her leaving at 11pm.
- One gap: the CCTV enhancement. Without it, the cardigan could be anyone's.

**Ch 23 | HELEN | "Doubt"**
Helen tests every alternative explanation. She finds none that holds.
- Rosalind brings soup. They sit together for an hour. Rosalind talks about the cathedral, her thirty years, what it means to her.
- Last line: "She is a good person who did a terrible thing. That is not a contradiction. That is the hardest kind of case."

**Ch 24 | EDMUND | "Pressure"**
Claire's superiors pressing for an arrest. The case against Webb is circumstantially strong externally — motive, evasion, presence in the close.
- Father Simon Reeve's testimony about the Webb-Marcus argument in the Chapter House two weeks before the death: Webb looks worse.
- Edmund knows what is coming. He cannot stop it without the CCTV result.

**Ch 25 | HELEN | "Convergence"**
Investigation publicly converging on Webb. The right person is disappearing from view.
- Helen at evensong. Rosalind conducts the anthem with entirely steady hands.
- Helen watches and knows: this is either innocence or extraordinary control. She knows which one it is.

**Ch 26 | EDMUND | "The Arrest"**
DS Claire arrests Canon Alistair Webb. The false accusation beat — 65% through the book.
- Edmund at the station. Webb is terrified in a way that reads to Edmund as genuine.
- Helen, with Claire's permission, visits Webb in the interview room. He is afraid. "Frightened people don't look like that unless they're innocent."
- 48 hours before Webb is charged. Edmund needs the CCTV enhancement now.

**Ch 27 | HELEN | "48 Hours"**
Helen pushes for the CCTV enhancement to be expedited. Technical delays.
- Rosalind calls on Helen. She has heard about Webb's arrest. Horrified. Offers to write a character reference.
- Helen cannot respond directly. She makes tea.
- Last line: "Helen made tea. She did not drink it."

**Ch 28 | EDMUND | "The Enhancement"**
CCTV enhancement confirmed: the cardigan is Rosalind's. Specific weave, collar style, consistent with garment known from multiple witness observations.
- **CLUE 5 full payoff:** The cardigan identified beyond reasonable doubt.
- **CLUE 2 full payoff:** Second digoxin bottle traced to a private pharmacist. Cash purchase. The date matches a withdrawal from the music fund.
- Helen and Edmund take everything to Claire. She is silent for a long time. "Show me everything."
- Webb released the same afternoon. He does not look at anyone as he leaves.

**Ch 29 | HELEN | "Redirected"**
Claire officially redirects the investigation to Rosalind. The arrest warrant process begins.
- The close does not yet know. Rosalind is still at her desk.
- Helen walks past No. 4 the morning after. Rosalind is at the window, watering a plant. Their eyes meet briefly. Neither speaks.

**Ch 30 | EDMUND | "The Final Piece"**
Everything assembled. The arrest warrant ready.
- Edmund sits with what this means: thirty years of devoted service. A decision made in one afternoon. A person he has worked beside, respected, trusted with tea and with institutional knowledge.
- He thinks about Rosalind bringing him tea while he worked on the fund audit. She was asking what he knew. She was assessing his progress. She was warm the whole time.
- Last line: "He closed his notebook. He would not open it in the close in quite the same way again."

### ACT III — THE COST (Chapters 31–40)

**Goal:** Arrest. Denouement. Aftermath. Series thread. The cathedral continues.

---

**Ch 31 | HELEN | "The Morning"**
Dawn on the day of the arrest. Helen in the archive — her place, her ordinary work.
- The cathedral at its most beautiful and most indifferent. Seven centuries of human behaviour absorbed without comment.
- Helen's interior — what it costs to be right about this particular person.

**Ch 32 | EDMUND | "No. 4"**
Claire arrests Rosalind at her house at No. 4. Edmund and Helen present — requested by Claire because familiar faces will keep it quieter.
- Rosalind opens the door. She looks at Edmund's face. She knows.
- She does not perform shock. She says: "Come in, then." She makes tea. The three of them sit in her front room, the piano behind her.
- The arrest is the frame. The denouement is the content.

**Ch 33 | HELEN | "Assembly" — DENOUEMENT BEAT 1**
Helen walks through every clue in order, quietly. Each one lands as a small confirmation.
- Rosalind listens. Does not deny. At each point, the slightest nod — yes, that was me.
- The reader thinks: I saw that. I didn't understand it. This is the reader's reward.
- Every clue accounted for. Every red herring retroactively explained.

**Ch 34 | EDMUND | "The Killer's Truth" — DENOUEMENT BEAT 2**
Rosalind speaks. Not a performance — honesty, possibly for the first time in twelve years.
- The care home fees. The small withdrawals. Marcus's discovery three months ago. The chance to repay quietly that he then withdrew. The afternoon she made the decision.
- "I would have lost everything I have given my life to."
- Edmund: "Did you think about what Marcus gave his life to?"
- Silence. Rosalind: "Every day since."
- Edmund and Helen feel something complicated here. Not sympathy exactly. Something closer to grief.

**Ch 35 | HELEN | "The Cost" — DENOUEMENT BEAT 3**
The arrest is formal. Claire reads the caution.
- Rosalind goes without protest. She picks up her cardigan — the pale blue one — and puts it on. She goes quietly.
- Edmund watches her leave No. 4 for the last time.
- The cost: the close will never be entirely the same. A person who devoted thirty years to this place did something unforgivable in it.

**Ch 36 | EDMUND | "Morning Prayer"**
The close the next morning. 7am Morning Prayer — thinner than usual, but present.
- Father Simon Reeve leads the responses in Rosalind's place. Imperfect. Real.
- Edmund in his usual stall. The cathedral continues. As it has for seven centuries.
- His institutional loyalty finally resolved: he chose truth. The institution survived it. He had not expected that.

**Ch 37 | HELEN | "Julian"**
Julian is leaving. They say goodbye differently than they have in two years.
- "You're good at this, Mum." She doesn't know what to do with that.
- The estrangement not erased — but a door no longer locked.
- Helen watches his car leave the close. Then turns back to the cathedral.

**Ch 38 | EDMUND | "The Close"**
Community aftermath in the days that follow.
- Webb: "Will this close ever be clean?" Edmund: "It never was. That's not what cathedrals are for."
- Father Reeve lost his naive faith in the close. He kept the real thing.
- Rose Hartley, at her desk in the song school — quiet, present, unremarkable. Background.

**Ch 39 | HELEN | "The Archive"**
Helen returns to her archive volunteer work. Returns to the files she has been avoiding since the investigation consumed her.
- She pulls the music fund query from twelve years ago — the note she found in Ch 4.
- Beneath the file folder: a letter from 1887. It does not belong with the others. It has been misfiled for a very long time.
- She reads it twice. Puts it face-down on her desk. Photographs it on her phone. Sends the photograph to Edmund.
- **SERIES THREAD planted — whisper, not revelation.** The chapter does not make a drama of this.

**Ch 40 | EDMUND | "Something in the Archive"**
Edmund reads the photograph on his phone. A payment made in 1887 to a family called Hartley — "in settlement of the matter of the east transept." Signed by the then Dean.
- He looks across the close to the song school. Rose Hartley is at the window.
- He calls Helen. "I think there's something in the archive."
- Helen: "I know. I've thought so for some time."
- Final image: Morning Prayer begins at 7am. The organ plays. The coloured light falls through the east window. The close continues — ancient, beautiful, not finished with them yet.

## NON-NEGOTIABLE WRITING RULES

1. Chapter 1 must hook by the end of page 1 — use French's technique: wrongness felt before wrongness named
2. Maximum 2 primary POVs — Helen and Edmund only. No other character's interiority, ever.
3. Every chapter must advance the mystery OR reveal character that changes the investigation
4. Chapter length: **2,000–2,200 words. Target 2,100. Hard ceiling 2,400.**
5. The killer (Rosalind) appears warmly in every chapter she enters — must be liked before she is exposed
6. Fair-play: every clue is visible to the careful reader before the reveal
7. The denouement uses the three-beat structure exactly: Assembly (Ch 33) → Killer's Truth (Ch 34) → The Cost (Ch 35)
