## THE SOLUTION — LOCKED (do not reveal until Chapter 32–35)

**Killer:** Canon Rosalind Ferris, 62, Precentor of Wychford Cathedral

**Method:** Digoxin overdose. Marcus Vayne took digoxin daily for his heart condition. Rosalind obtained a second bottle through a private pharmacist (cash, no prescription). She dissolved a lethal dose into Marcus's evening coffee, brought to the organ room under the pretext of discussing a rehearsal. Marcus lost consciousness at the organ within 30–45 minutes. Rosalind pushed his unconscious body from the organ gallery to simulate a fall. The fall obscured the true cause of death until toxicology confirmed digoxin at a level inconsistent with his prescribed dose.

**Motive:** Twelve years ago, Rosalind began making small withdrawals from the cathedral music fund to cover care home fees for her mother, who had dementia. Marcus discovered this three months before the story begins. He gave her the opportunity to repay quietly. He then withdrew that offer and insisted on disclosure to the Chapter. She would have lost her position, pension, home, and thirty years of reputation. The murder was panic, not premeditation — she made the decision over the course of one afternoon.

**Opportunity window:** Rosalind visited the organ room at approximately 9:30pm on the night of the murder. Julian Marsh witnessed Marcus leaving her house at that time (Ch 11). She had access via her Precentor's key and her Song School key.

**Her constructed alibi:** Claims she was home all evening. No witnesses. The alibi is never tested until late in the investigation.

**Her fatal mistakes:**
1. She did not know Thomas Oakes was doing his rounds 30 minutes late — he saw her leaving the organ loft at 11pm in her pale blue cardigan
2. She lied about the reason for her visit (the Thursday rehearsal that didn't exist) — Helen caught this within 24 hours
3. The Song School corridor CCTV recorded her accessing the archive storeroom to plant Marcus's medication bottle

## FALSE ACCUSATION BEAT

**Chapter:** 26 (~65% through book)

**Falsely accused:** Canon Alistair Webb

**Evidence against him at that point:**
- Long-standing motive (Director of Music loss, 15 years of resentment)
- Alibi confirmed by Sarah Clifton but affair not yet public — his account inconsistent
- Seen in the close area the night of the murder
- Father Simon Reeve's testimony: heated argument between Webb and Marcus in the Chapter House two weeks before the murder
- Behaviour throughout the inquiry: evasive, hostile

**What the detective (Claire) gets wrong:**
She prioritises the behavioural evidence and the motive history over the access question. She does not yet know the Song School key significance. Pressure from superiors to close the case is real.

**What breaks it:**
Ch 28: CCTV enhancement confirms Rosalind's cardigan. Second digoxin bottle traced to private pharmacist. The cash withdrawal from the music fund matches. Webb's access to the archive storeroom is impossible — he has never held a Song School key.

**Emotional weight:**
When Helen visits Webb in the interview room (Ch 26), she sees genuine terror. "Frightened people don't look like that unless they're innocent." This is the moment the reader fully abandons Webb as a suspect.
