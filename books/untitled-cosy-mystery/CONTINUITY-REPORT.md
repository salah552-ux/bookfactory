## CRITICAL CONFLICTS — FIX BEFORE APPROVAL

### Conflict C1: Julian Marsh's alibi is internally contradictory

**Type:** Factual / Timeline
**Books affected:** Death in the Cathedral Close — Chapters 7 and 11 (FACTS.md lines 188, 258–260)

**Chapter 7 / Line 188 states:**
> "Julian's pub the night of the death: King's Head; two pints, ~9–11pm."

**Chapter 11 / Lines 258–260 state:**
> "Julian's confirmed alibi: King's Head pub approximately 9pm to last orders (~11pm), two pints."
> "Julian walked back through the close gate at approximately 9:30pm using Helen's spare gate key (which he still has)."
> "CRITICAL TIMELINE FACT: Julian saw Marcus at approximately 9:30pm leaving Rosalind's house, walking steadily toward the north transept direction."

**What's wrong:** The alibi states Julian was at the King's Head from approximately 9pm to last orders (~11pm). Last orders at an English pub is typically 11pm. If Julian was at the pub until 11pm, he cannot have been walking through the close gate at 9:30pm — 90 minutes earlier. These two facts, as written, cannot both be true. This matters critically because the 9:30pm sighting of Marcus leaving Rosalind's house is the pivotal piece of testimony that dismantles Rosalind's alibi; it must be delivered by a credible witness with a coherent timeline.

**Recommended fix:** Change the alibi language in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 11 entries to establish that Julian left the pub around 9–9:30pm (not "to last orders"):

- Line 188: Change `~9–11pm` to `approximately 7–9:30pm`
- Line 258: Change `approximately 9pm to last orders (~11pm), two pints` to `approximately 7pm, departed around 9:30pm, two pints`

This is consistent with "two pints" (a plausible 2–2.5 hour pub visit), gives Julian a partial but meaningful alibi, and keeps his 9:30pm close-gate arrival intact as a plot-critical fact. The CLUE-MAP already describes his alibi as "partial" — this fix aligns the FACTS.md with that existing characterisation.

**FACTS.md update needed:** Yes — lines 188 and 258.
