## 2. FIRST-LINE TREATMENT — THE 2024 ACG STANDARD (locked)

**The headline fact the book is built to deliver correctly:**

- **Optimised bismuth quadruple therapy (BQT) is the preferred first-line treatment** under the 2024 ACG guideline.
  - Composition: **PPI + bismuth + a nitroimidazole (e.g. metronidazole) + tetracycline.**
  - Typical course length: 14 days (state as "usually 14 days," defer exact dosing to the prescriber — see compliance note).
- The 2024 ACG guideline **removed clarithromycin triple therapy and concomitant therapy as recommended first-line options.**
- The guideline **advises avoiding clarithromycin-based and levofloxacin-based regimens unless susceptibility testing confirms they will work** (because of resistance stats #2 and #4).

**Source:** ACG 2024 clinical guideline summaries + guideline PDF (MARKET-INTELLIGENCE.md SOURCES — gi.org, pharmacy.umn.edu, physiciansweekly.com, acg_clinical_guideline PDF).

**Why this matters for the reader (manuscript framing):** Many readers were given clarithromycin triple therapy (the old standard) by a GP who has not updated to the 2024 guideline — which is part of why their re-test came back positive. The book explains the shift so the reader can have an informed conversation with their doctor. It does NOT tell the reader which drugs to take — that is the prescriber's decision.

## 3. WHEN IT FAILS — THE RESCUE PATHWAY (the differentiator lane)

This is the chapter no incumbent consumer title covers. Every claim here is sourced.

- **Second-line, if first-line was NOT bismuth quadruple:** bismuth quadruple therapy is the preferred second-line.
- **Rifabutin-based rescue:** rifabutin 150 mg + amoxicillin 1 g + PPI, twice daily, for 14 days — an established regimen for difficult-to-treat patients.
  - Source: PMC6406425 (MARKET-INTELLIGENCE.md SOURCES).
- **Furazolidone-based rescue:** documented to achieve eradication even after as many as five prior failed attempts with multi-resistant strains.
  - Source: PMC8467492 (MARKET-INTELLIGENCE.md SOURCES).
- **Core honest message (stat #6):** 27–41% of failures happen even when the bacteria are NOT resistant. Adherence (finishing the full course), correct PPI dosing/timing, and not stopping early are decisive. Resistance is not the whole story.

**Compliance note:** Present rescue regimens as *what the evidence and guidelines describe* and *what to ask your gastroenterologist about* — never as a self-administered protocol. Dosages are quoted from the literature for the reader's informed conversation, with an explicit "your doctor prescribes this, not this book" line each time a regimen is named.

## 5. POST-TREATMENT CONFIRMATION — EXACT WORDING (locked)

The book's answer to the verbatim reader question "how do I make sure it's actually gone?"

- **Test of cure is essential.** Symptom relief is NOT proof of eradication.
- **Stool antigen test OR urea breath test**, performed **at least 4 weeks after the last antibiotic dose.**
- **Stop PPIs about 2 weeks before the test** — PPIs suppress the bacteria and can produce a false-negative.
- A serology (blood antibody) test is NOT a test of cure — antibodies persist after the infection clears. Do not let the reader confuse "I had a positive blood test before treatment" with re-test.
- **Source:** ACG 2024 guideline test-of-cure standard.

This is stat #11. It is the practical payoff of the "still positive after antibiotics" validation that opens the book.

## 7. THE STOMACH-LINING DIET (locked food framework)

The book's diet chapter is a symptom-load-reduction and lining-support framework, NOT a cure diet.

- **Reduce / approach with care:** very salty foods, cured and smoked meats, excess coffee, alcohol, and (for symptomatic individuals) highly acidic or spicy foods that worsen burning/gnawing. Salt and cured/smoked meats carry an independent association with gastric-cancer risk — frame factually, not as fear.
- **Support:** easily digestible proteins, cooked vegetables, soluble fibre, fermented foods in small amounts (tolerance-dependent), adequate hydration spaced around meals.
- **Mechanism over rules:** explain *why* (acid load, mucosal irritation, the inflamed lining needs quiet) before listing what to change — matches series voice.
- **Compliance:** "may reduce symptom load," "may help your stomach lining settle" — never "heals your ulcer," never "cures gastritis."
- **No numeric diet claims** without a source entered here.
