### TOOL 1 — The questions, ready for the room

Lead with the ones that fit where you are. You don't need to ask all of them.

**If you're newly diagnosed:**
- Given the 2024 guidance, is optimised bismuth quadruple therapy right for me?
- Exactly how and when do I take each tablet — especially the acid-suppressing one, relative to meals? (Ask for it in writing.)
- How long is the course, and why does finishing all of it matter?

**If you're still positive after treatment:**
- What did I take, and what's the right next step now it's failed?
- Can we do susceptibility testing before choosing the next regimen?
- Should I be referred to a gastroenterologist?

**For everyone:**
- For my test of cure: can it be a stool or breath test — not a blood test — at least 4 weeks after the antibiotics, and off the acid tablets for ~2 weeks first?
- As it can cluster in households, should anyone in my family be tested?

> **If you only have nerve for one question, make it the right one:** newly diagnosed → "Am I getting the current first-line treatment?" · still positive → "Can I have a referral and susceptibility testing?" · finished treatment → "When do I do a test of cure?"

---

### TOOL 2 — How to know it's actually gone

Symptom relief is not proof. Confirm it properly:

- **Use a stool antigen test OR a urea breath test.** Not a blood (antibody) test — antibodies can stay positive for years after you've cleared it, so blood is not a test of cure.
- **At least 4 weeks** after your last antibiotic dose.
- **Stop PPIs about 2 weeks before** the test — they can suppress the bacteria and give a false "all clear." (Ask your doctor before stopping anything.)

A quick way to work out your date:
**Last antibiotic ____ + 4 weeks = earliest test date ____ · stop PPI ~2 weeks before ____**

---

### Seek urgent care — don't wait — for any of these:

Blood in your stool or vomit · black or tarry stools · vomit like coffee grounds · unexplained weight loss · difficulty swallowing · persistent vomiting. These are alarm features that need a doctor straight away.
