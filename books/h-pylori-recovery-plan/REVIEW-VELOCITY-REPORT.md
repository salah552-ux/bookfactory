## DIMENSION ASSESSMENT (task-specified)

**Reader-promise delivery — PASS.** The KDP core promise (understand the 2024 treatment, know what to do when it fails, see cancer risk in proportion, confirm eradication, never be sold a supplement) is delivered chapter-for-chapter. The "when antibiotics fail" rescue pathway (Ch.5/file 07) is the genuine differentiator and is fully built out — second-line logic, susceptibility testing, rifabutin, furazolidone-after-five-failures, all routed through a gastroenterologist.

**Voice consistency — PASS.** Warm, authoritative, plain English, second person throughout. Contractions consistent. Mechanism-before-instruction is honoured in every chapter. No forbidden phrases detected (no "journey," no "miracle/game-changing," no "Additionally/Furthermore/Moreover," no "be kind to yourself"). The mirror-sentence register from FACTS.md §9 is present in the Introduction and echoed in the Conclusion.

**Structure — PASS.** The 4-phase reader journey (de-panic → informed treatment → rescue plan → trust/closure) lands clearly. Note: the *book sells a "4-phase protocol" feel* via the one-page plan's nine steps + three-irreducible-core; the structure is coherent and reader-owned.

**Avatar fit (Rachel) — PASS, strong.** Every one of Rachel's four ranked fears (cancer, still-positive, reinfection/family, contagion) gets a dedicated, proportionate answer. Her verbatim Google queries are mirrored as chapter hooks ("what am I allowed to eat?", "how do I make sure it's gone?", "did I give it to my family?"). Her distrust of sellers is explicitly validated rather than dismissed.

**Actionability — PASS.** Every chapter closes with a "What this means for you" takeaway box (consistent format, 4–5 bullets). Appendix B is a printable, tickable doctor checklist split by reader situation. Ch.11 distils to nine steps and a three-item core.

**No-panic / no-supplement-sales compliance — PASS (the book's highest-risk axis, cleared).** The cancer chapter never states a fear statistic without immediately pairing the de-panic number (NNT 332 / RR 0.61) — verified line by line. No brand, product, tea, honey, or vendor is named anywhere. The adjunct chapter (Ch.7) actively defuses the Matula/Manuka/"60-day natural cure" cluster as categories without naming them. The "nothing to sell you" stance is reinforced as the structural trust mechanism, not a tagline.

**Chapter balance — PASS.** No chapter is thin or padded. The longest reassurance passages (Ch.2 cancer, Ch.9 reinfection) earn their length because de-escalation is the product. Total 45,044 words sits at the lower end of the 45–55k range — deliberate per BLUEPRINT §5 ("a range, not a floor; quality over padding"). No chapter needed cutting or expansion.

## TOP OBSERVATIONS (not blocking — A-grade polish notes)

1. **[Metric 11 — KDP Readiness] Internal chapter-number vs file-name offset.** The manuscript H1 headers number chapters 1–12 matching the Table of Contents (e.g. "Chapter 2 — The Cancer Question"), while filenames run `04-chapter-03.md` etc. This is an internal-manifest quirk only; the reader-facing numbering in the headers and the front-matter Contents is correct and self-consistent. No reader impact. Left as-is; flagged for the production/build agent to confirm file-order assembly follows the numeric filename prefix (00→14, then 99), which it does.

2. **[Metric 4 — Pacing] Reassurance density.** A few chapters (notably Ch.9 reinfection) restate "uncommon, not impossible" and the "uncommon-but-confirm-properly" point more than once. This is a deliberate de-panic technique for an anxious avatar and reads as reinforcement rather than padding, but it is the single softest pacing edge. Not corrected — removing it would weaken the calming function for the target reader.

3. **[Metric 3 — Hook] Two chapter openings are quieter** (Ch.10 rebuild, Ch.11 plan) — appropriate, since both are post-crisis landing chapters where calm is the intended register. No fix warranted.

## WHAT'S WORKING (do not change)

- **The dual-message discipline** (cancer chapter): the seatbelt and sunscreen analogies make NNT 332 *felt*, not just read — this is the chapter the whole book was built to get right, and it is right.
- **The strain-naming restraint** (Ch.7): teaching the reader the species-vs-strain caveat instead of asserting a DSM number is both factually correct and a trust-builder.
- **The refusal to invent a reinfection percentage** (Ch.9): the manuscript turns an evidence gap into a trust signal ("when you notice a health source refusing to invent precision it does not have, that is usually a sign you can believe the precise things it does tell you").
- **The doctor-conversation architecture** (Ch.11 + Ch.12 + Appendix B): converts the entire book into a single prepared appointment — maximum actionability for the avatar.
