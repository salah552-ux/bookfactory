### Field-level synthesis
The consumer H. pylori book field is **thin and quality-stratified**: a long-tail evergreen (Hompes) anchored on an anti-antibiotic "natural in 60 days" promise, plus a cluster of fast-produced "Dr [Name]" / "Made Easy" / "Vanquished" self-pub guides competing on breadth and authority-framing rather than sourced evidence. **Three gaps are open across the entire field:**
1. **The "when antibiotics fail" pathway** — almost no consumer title addresses second-line / rescue therapy despite a ~28% failure rate.
2. **Proportionate, sourced cancer-risk framing** — competitors either ignore cancer or fear-sell it; none defuse the anxiety with the actual numbers.
3. **Trust through restraint** — the field is saturated with "cure" promises and supplement-adjacent upsell. A guide that explicitly refuses to sell supplements and cites named sources owns a position no incumbent holds.

## 4b. READER LANGUAGE HARVEST

Captured from forum/community and patient-site search snippets (r/HPylori, HealthUnlocked, patient.info, nutrition blogs). These phrases go directly into copy and metadata.

### How they describe symptoms
- "burning" / "burning in my stomach"
- "gnawing" / "gnawing pain"
- "empty stomach pain" / "worse when I haven't eaten" / "pain between meals"
- "nausea" / "constant nausea"
- "acid reflux" / "reflux that won't go away"
- "bloating" / "full after a few bites"
- "metallic taste" (often during/after antibiotics)

### What they fear most
- "Am I going to get cancer?" — the dominant anxiety. (H. pylori-positive patients show significantly higher measured anxiety scores than negative patients — confirmed in clinical literature.)
- "stomach cancer" / "is this going to turn into cancer"
- "will it come back?" (reinfection fear)
- "is it contagious — did I give it to my family?"

### What frustrates them
- "doctor gave me antibiotics and I'm still positive"
- "second round and still positive"
- "the side effects are awful" (nausea, diarrhoea, metallic taste from the antibiotics)
- "my doctor didn't re-test me / told me not to worry"
- "I don't know what I'm allowed to eat"
- distrust of sellers — "is the Matula tea / Manuka honey thing a scam?"

### What they want
- "natural alternatives" / "natural treatment" / "without antibiotics" (a real segment — but our book reframes this as adjuncts, not replacement)
- "diet that helps" / "what can I eat" / "foods to avoid"
- "how to heal my stomach lining"
- "how do I make sure it's actually gone" (re-test / confirmation of eradication)
- "what to do when antibiotics don't work"
- reassurance that is honest, not dismissive

**Copy implication:** The book and listing must open on the test-result + cancer-Google moment, validate the "still positive after antibiotics" frustration explicitly, and signal restraint (no supplement sales) early. "Gnawing," "burning," "empty stomach pain," "still positive," and "am I going to get cancer" are the exact verbatim phrases to mirror.

## 4c. 2025 CLINICAL LANDSCAPE

### First-line treatment (ACG 2024 guidelines)
- **Optimised bismuth quadruple therapy (BQT) is now the preferred first-line option.** Composition: a PPI + bismuth + a nitroimidazole + tetracycline.
- The 2024 ACG guideline **removed clarithromycin triple therapy and concomitant therapy** as first-line recommendations, and advises **avoiding clarithromycin- and levofloxacin-based regimens unless susceptibility testing confirms they will work.**

### Resistance rates
- **Clarithromycin resistance in the US: 22.2%–31.5%** (cited in ACG 2024).
- Other commonly reported resistance: metronidazole ~43.2%, levofloxacin ~33.9%, clarithromycin ~24% (regional study figures).
- **Treatment failure: ~28.7% of patients fail**, and a substantial share (27–41%) **fail despite being sensitive to the antibiotics used** — i.e., resistance does not fully explain failure (adherence, PPI dosing, and other factors matter).

### When it fails (rescue)
- **Bismuth quadruple therapy is the preferred second-line** when first-line fails.
- **Rifabutin-based rescue** (rifabutin 150 mg + amoxicillin 1 g + PPI BID × 14 days) is established for difficult-to-treat patients.
- **Furazolidone-based rescue** is documented even after five prior eradication failures with multi-resistant strains.

### Cancer risk framing (the chapter that must be exactly right)
- **IARC / WHO (press release No. 368, 7 July 2025)** + **Nature Medicine (Sep 2025, article s41591-025-03793-6):** globally, **~15.6 million lifetime gastric cancer cases** are expected within the 2008–2017 birth cohorts, of which **~76% are attributable to H. pylori** and therefore potentially preventable. Regional split: ~8M Asia, ~1.5M Americas, ~1.4M Africa.
- **H. pylori is a Group 1 (definite) carcinogen** per IARC.
- **Eradication reduces gastric cancer risk:** updated meta-analysis RR 0.61 (95% CI 0.47–0.79); **NNT ≈ 332** — i.e., eradication meaningfully lowers risk, but the absolute individual risk for most carriers remains low. This is the dual message: the risk is real enough to treat, low enough not to panic.

### Natural / adjunct interventions with 2024–2025 evidence
- **Mastic gum (Chios):** ~92.2% eradication when combined with triple therapy vs ~63.3% antibiotics alone (per cited figures); **caveat — it needs an acidic environment and is undermined by PPIs**, so timing matters. Adjunct, not standalone.
- **Broccoli-sprout sulforaphane:** 2024 systematic review (Frontiers in Medicine) — real potential to reduce H. pylori colonisation as an adjunct, but **did not fully clear infection by breath-test standards** in key trials. Adjunct only.
- **Lactobacillus reuteri (esp. DSM 17648):** 2025 meta-analysis — adding L. reuteri to triple therapy raises eradication rate vs triple therapy alone, and improves side-effect tolerance. Adjunct, well-evidenced for tolerability.
- **Headline honesty line for the book:** natural approaches alone typically eradicate only ~10–40% vs ~80%+ for guideline antibiotic regimens. Frame every adjunct as "alongside," never "instead of."
