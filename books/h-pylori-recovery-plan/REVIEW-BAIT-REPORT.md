## CURRENT AMAZON REVIEW GUIDELINES (verified by WebSearch this session, June 2026)

Key rules confirmed:
- Authors **may** ask readers for an **honest** review. Permitted.
- Authors **may** give free/discounted copies (ARCs) **only** if no review is required in exchange and no attempt is made to influence content or sentiment. Reviewer must disclose a free copy.
- **Prohibited:** incentivised reviews (free product/refund/gift card/payment in exchange for a review — banned since Oct 2016, unchanged); quid-pro-quo review swaps ("I'll review yours if you review mine"); any language that nudges sentiment (the ask must be neutral, not "leave a positive review").
- Linking to the product/review page from within the book is permitted (placeholder URL approach is standard practice).

Sources:
- https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G202101910 (Customer Reviews)
- https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=G8CXDFT9GLRRSV3G (Anti-Manipulation Policy)
- https://pubnook.com/article/amazon-book-review-policy-2026-what-authors-can--cant-do

**Compliance status of existing CTAs: PASS (all four).** Every prompt asks only for an *honest* review, attaches no incentive, and never nudges toward positive sentiment. The recurring phrase "if the book earned it" is exemplary — it explicitly conditions the ask on honest assessment rather than soliciting praise.

## CHANGES APPLIED

1. **Back-matter CTA — Beat 1 strengthened.** Added one acknowledgment sentence-pair before the ask:
   > You came to this book frightened, at 2am, with a search engine making everything worse — and you are leaving it with the fear in proportion, a plan in hand, and the questions that turn a diagnosis into a path forward. That is real work, and you did it. Before you close the book, there is one small thing only you can do.
   This deepens the Beat 1 → Beat 2 → Beat 3 structure and earns the ask. Reader-centred, in-voice, fully compliant.

2. **Introduction prompt — removed (premature ask at the fear-peak).** The original Introduction contained a soft review ask placed at the book's most fragile emotional moment (right after "close the search tab"), *before* the book had delivered any value. Per the spec ("the CTA should come after the reader has completed the emotional arc — never before"), an ask here risks a tonal intrusion that costs trust the later, better-placed asks rely on. Replaced with a pure forward-pull that protects the emotional landing:
   > Nothing beyond the next page needs you tonight — not the appointment, not the treatment, not even this book. There is only one thing to do right now, and it is to keep reading. Everything else can wait until the fear is smaller, which it is about to be.
   (Checked against forbidden-phrases: an initial draft using "Don't worry" was corrected — that phrase is on the book's NEVER list as the useless thing doctors say.) Also reordered so the Introduction ends on the strong launch line "Let's start with what is actually living in your stomach."

**Review-prompt architecture after edits (the correct velocity shape — ask after value lands, never before):**
- Ch.8 (after the "knowing it's gone" payoff) — kept, well-placed.
- Ch.12 (after the transformation beat) — kept, well-placed.
- Back-matter "A Small, Honest Request" (after the full emotional arc) — strengthened.
- Introduction premature ask — removed.
