## 1. COMPLETE CONTENT

Title: *The H. Pylori Stomach-Lining Food Card*
Subtitle: *What to eat while your stomach settles — and how to know it's gone.*

Anchor strip (highlighted): "No food clears H. pylori — antibiotics do. Eating well reduces the load on an inflamed lining and keeps you comfortable. That's the whole job: support, not cure."

Two-column food table —

REDUCE (mostly while symptoms are active):
- Very salty foods, cured & smoked meats (an independent long-term stomach-cancer link — calm fact, not a scare)
- Excess coffee and alcohol (can stimulate acid)
- Highly acidic or spicy foods — only if they personally set off your burning

SUPPORT (gentle on a raw lining):
- Easily digestible proteins (eggs, fish, tender poultry, pulses if tolerated)
- Cooked (not raw) vegetables
- Soluble fibre (oats, cooked carrots, banana)
- Small amounts of fermented food, if tolerated
- Steady hydration, spaced around meals

THE ONE QUESTION (highlighted strip): "Is this likely to irritate a raw, inflamed surface, or feed the inflammation? If yes, ease off it for now. If no, it's probably fine. You're the expert on your own stomach."

HOW TO EAT box (small): "Smaller, more frequent meals so acid isn't left against an empty stomach · eat slowly, don't overfill · don't lie down straight after your largest meal."

HOW TO KNOW IT'S GONE box:
- "Use a stool antigen OR urea breath test."
- "Do it at least 4 weeks after your last antibiotic."
- "Stop PPIs about 2 weeks before the test (ask your doctor)."
- "A blood (antibody) test is NOT a test of cure — it can stay positive after you've cleared it."
- "Symptom relief alone is not proof."

Footer disclaimer: "Diet supports comfort; it does not replace treatment. Not medical advice — treatment is prescribed and supervised by your doctor. Seek urgent care for blood in stool/vomit, black/tarry stools, unexplained weight loss, difficulty swallowing, or persistent vomiting."
