You finished the week. You named your trigger. But a gut doesn't read the calendar — a flare can still arrive on an ordinary Tuesday. This card is for that moment. Keep it on your phone or pin it to the fridge. It takes about a minute.

## Step 1 — Which trigger is firing today? (30 seconds)

Ask three questions, in order. Stop at the first clear "yes."

- **Did symptoms follow a meal within roughly 20–90 minutes today?** → likely **Food** is firing.
- **Did symptoms follow a stressful hour or event, with no clear food link?** → likely **Stress** is firing.
- **Are symptoms timing-independent — not clearly after food or stress, just the familiar too-slow or too-fast pattern?** → likely **Motility** is firing.

Most people are primarily one profile. On a flare day, you're identifying which mechanism is loudest *right now* — not re-diagnosing yourself.

## Step 2 — Your first move (pick the row that matches Step 1)

| Today's trigger | First move (do this first) |
|-----------------|----------------------------|
| **Food** | Return to one or two of your Calm Plate default meals for the rest of the day. Reduce high-fermentable foods. Don't add anything new. |
| **Stress** | Do the Stress Bridge now: four minutes — two minutes of the double-inhale, slow-exhale breath, then two minutes of low humming. Eat your next meal sitting down, without multitasking. |
| **Motility** | Adjust the lever you know works for your pattern: slow → more soluble fibre and water, a short walk after your next meal; fast → ease off insoluble fibre and known accelerators for the day. |

## Step 3 — The one-day reset rule

A single flare day is information, not a setback. Catch it early — at your first warning signal — and one day on your Calm Plate defaults is usually enough to settle it. You don't have to repeat the whole week. You already built the map; this is just reading it.

One honest note: this card is for the everyday flare, not for symptoms that are severe, new, or persistent. If something feels different or won't settle, see a doctor. This is not a substitute for medical advice.

You did the hard part already — you learned what's actually happening. This card is here for the moments when you just need to remember the answer quickly.
