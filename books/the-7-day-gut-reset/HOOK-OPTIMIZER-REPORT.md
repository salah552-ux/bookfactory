### Chapter 03-day-three — Opening
**Score:** 34/50 | **Primary weakness:** Specificity (3/10), Promise tension (5/10)
**Original:**
> You know your profile. Now the protocol starts — and it starts with what you eat, and how.

**Weakness:** This is a transition sentence, not a hook. It tells the reader what's about to happen without giving them a reason to feel anything about it. "You know your profile" is a recap. "Now the protocol starts" is a chapter heading. There is no image, no tension, no specific detail that couldn't belong to any self-help book in any category. The reader is already inside the book — they need to feel the next chapter pulling them, not a stage direction.

**Technique applied:** Forward promise — names what the next three days will produce, framed as something the reader now realises they need, with a concrete time unit that makes the commitment feel achievable.

**Rewrite:**
> Three days of a quieter plate. That's what stands between where you are now and the first clear signal your gut has sent in months.

**Why this works better:** "Three days" is specific and commits to a timeline. "The first clear signal your gut has sent in months" names the outcome in the reader's emotional language — not "symptom reduction" but the experience of clarity after months of noise. The reader who has had a reactive gut for months feels this immediately. One sentence does the work of the original two, with more pull.

### Chapter 03-day-three — Closing
**Score:** 36/50 | **Primary weakness:** Emotional resonance (5/10), Review intent (5/10)
**Original:**
> Three days of a quieter plate gives your gut the signal it cannot get while everything is turned up to full volume.

**Weakness:** Good imagery, but "turned up to full volume" is slightly abstract and the closing doesn't give the reader a reason to move to Day 4. It resolves the chapter but creates no forward pull. The "signal" metaphor is doing work but the closing sentence feels like a tagline rather than a landing.

**Technique applied:** Resolution + pull — closes the chapter's promise (the plate quiets the gut) and opens the reason Day 4 matters (the quiet enables what comes next to work).

**Rewrite:**
> Your gut has been working against a constant background load. Three days of a quieter plate removes that load long enough for the pattern to become visible — and for what follows to actually land.

**Why this works better:** "Background load" is precise and recognisable to anyone with a chronically reactive gut. "Long enough for the pattern to become visible" confirms this is temporary (addressing Day 3's compliance anxiety), and "for what follows to actually land" creates forward pull toward the Repair Kit without naming it — the reader wants to know what "what follows" means.

### Chapter 04-day-four — Opening
**Score:** 33/50 | **Primary weakness:** Reader recognition (4/10), Immediacy (5/10)
**Original:**
> Yesterday was about reducing what's creating noise. Today is about adding what helps the gut do its job.

**Weakness:** This is a chapter summary, not a hook. It tells the reader what both chapters are about in general terms, but gives them nothing to feel. "Adding what helps the gut do its job" is vague — it could describe any Day 4 in any gut health book. The reader has just been through a restriction chapter (Day 3) and arrives at Day 4 with some anxiety and some relief. This opening doesn't acknowledge either state.

**Technique applied:** Reader recognition mirror — names the emotional shift the reader is actually experiencing at this point in the book (moving from restriction to addition), without performing it.

**Rewrite:**
> Restriction was yesterday's work. Today you add — and adding feels different to most people than taking away does.

**Why this works better:** "Restriction was yesterday's work" closes that chapter definitively. "Adding feels different to most people than taking away does" names a real psychological truth — the reader who has just spent a day reducing things will recognise this as describing their actual experience. It creates a small mystery ("different how?") that pulls them into the chapter without a rhetorical question.

### Chapter 05-day-five — Closing
**Score:** 38/50 | **Primary weakness:** Review intent (5/10), Quotability (5/10)
**Original:**
> The Stress Bridge doesn't require you to feel less stressed — it requires you to activate a different system while the stress is still happening.

**Weakness:** This is a strong line but it ends on the mechanism rather than on the reader. It closes the chapter correctly but the final sentence is slightly clinical. The book's voice at its best is warmer than this — the reader has just been given something practical and unusual, and the closing should acknowledge the significance of that more directly while staying in the book's register.

**Technique applied:** The quotable line — one sentence that stands alone, distilling the chapter's argument to its sharpest point in the reader's language.

**Rewrite:**
> The Stress Bridge works whether you believe in it or not. Four minutes. The system responds to the signal — not to your confidence in the process.

**Why this works better:** "Works whether you believe in it or not" directly addresses the reader's most common objection (which has already been pre-empted in the chapter body) but states it as a closing truth rather than a defensive hedge. "The system responds to the signal — not to your confidence in the process" is the chapter's core argument distilled to one sentence that a reader could pull out of context and it would still hold. The short sentence "Four minutes." mid-sequence creates rhythm and emphasis.

### Chapter 07-day-seven — Opening
**Score:** 28/50 | **Primary weakness:** CRITICAL FAILURE — Specificity (3/10), Immediacy (3/10), Promise tension (4/10)
**Original:**
> You made it to Day 7. More than that — you made it through seven days with data, not guesswork.

**Weakness:** "You made it" is the most generic possible opening for a final chapter — it appears in some form in nearly every day-by-day programme book in the health category. "More than that" is a transition construction, not an arresting opener. "Data, not guesswork" is the book's thesis but at Day 7 the reader already knows this — restating it as an arrival point produces no tension, no recognition, no forward pull. The reader at Day 7 deserves an opening that matches where they actually are: not just "done" but holding something specific they've built.

**Technique applied:** Precision reframe — names where the reader started and where they are now in specific, concrete terms that make the gap between those two states feel significant. No preamble. No congratulation. Just the accurate account of what changed.

**Rewrite:**
> Seven days ago you had a gut that wasn't responding. Today you have a name for why, a protocol calibrated to that reason, and data you built yourself. That gap is not small.

**Why this works better:** "A gut that wasn't responding" is the reader's Day 1 emotional state in precise language. "A name for why" is the Day 2 payoff. "A protocol calibrated to that reason" is Days 3–5. "Data you built yourself" is the maintenance map. The entire arc is compressed into two sentences, and "that gap is not small" lands as earned understatement — the opposite of the gushing congratulation the reader expects and would roll their eyes at. This is the book's voice at its best.
