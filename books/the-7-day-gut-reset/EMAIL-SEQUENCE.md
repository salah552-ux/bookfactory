## EMAIL 2 — VALUE
**Subject:** The reintroduction mistake that costs people their results
**Preview text:** One food at a time, 48 hours apart. Here's why the gap matters more than you'd think.
**Send:** 48 hours after sign-up

There's one part of the reset people rush, and it's the part that does the most work: reintroduction.

Here's the trap. After a few calmer days, it's tempting to bring several foods back at once — a bit of dairy, some bread, the legumes you missed. Then symptoms flare, and you've learned nothing, because you can't tell which food did it. You're back to guessing, which is exactly where you started.

The fix is two rules you already met on Day 6, worth saying again because they're easy to shortcut:

One food category at a time. Not two. Not "mostly one."

Forty-eight hours before the next. Your gut's response to a new input can take up to a day to fully show. Forty-eight hours gives you a clean window to read it — has symptom intensity risen three or more points compared with your calm days? That's your answer.

Reintroduce the least likely triggers first — mildly fermentable vegetables before dairy, dairy before wheat. Early wins build confidence before you test the harder suspects. And a reaction isn't a failure; it's a finding. You note it, you stop, you move on. One meal of something your gut doesn't love won't undo your week.

Do this properly and you don't just feel better — you walk away knowing what your gut actually tolerates. That knowledge is yours to keep.

More soon.
