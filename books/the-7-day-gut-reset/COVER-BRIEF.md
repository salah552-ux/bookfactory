## 1. MARKET ANALYSIS

### What the top competing covers do

The gut health short-guide category on Amazon UK/US (top 20 titles from COMPETITIVE-ANALYSIS.md scan, 2026-05-13) shows a visually homogeneous field:

- **Colour territory:** 14 of 20 covers use clinical blue or teal backgrounds. This has become the default signal for "gut health book" — which also means it is invisible against itself in a search results grid.
- **Imagery pattern:** Food photography dominates. Bowls of salad, fermented jars, vegetables arranged on white surfaces. The second most common imagery type is anatomical — digestive tract diagrams, gut microbiome illustrations. Both signal "another diet book."
- **Typography:** Rounded sans-serif in white or pale text, often light-weight. Reads as approachable but also lightweight — no sense of authority or specificity.
- **Title treatment:** Most titles are centred, occupy the top third of the cover, medium-weight. Subtitle text is small and secondary. The commercial implication is that every title in the category looks like a subtitle.
- **What's overused:** Blue/green gradient backgrounds, food bowls, probiotics imagery, gut silhouette diagrams, supplement bottles.

### The visual gap this cover will own

Zero competitors in the scanned top 20 use:
- Warm amber, ochre, or earth-tone palettes (source: COMPETITIVE-ANALYSIS.md Section 5 — cover direction brief, confirmed: "No food photography. Warm amber/ochre tones.")
- A tracking or journaling visual element (notebook, log, pen) — which directly signals the diagnostic-first promise
- Bold, high-contrast title treatment that reads at 160px thumbnail width
- A design that looks more like a precision self-help title (think finance or productivity) than a wellness/spa category book

This is the visual position available: **warm earth tones + journaling/tracking aesthetic + bold, confident typography + clinical authority without clinical coldness.**

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION STATEMENT

**Concept:** A cover that communicates personal precision — this book is about *your* gut, not everyone's gut. The visual language should suggest a working document, a personal record, something the reader will make their own — while carrying the authority of a title that knows what it's doing.

**Mood references:**
1. *Why We Sleep* by Matthew Walker (W&N, 2017 edition) — the dark background, bold clean title treatment, single striking visual element. Authority without academic stuffiness.
2. *The Happiness Advantage* by Shawn Achor — warm ochre palette, clean sans-serif confidence, no fussy imagery. Feels like a decision made, not a suggestion offered.
3. *Atomic Habits* by James Clear (2018, Avery) — the iconic orange, bold compressed title, minimal imagery. The cover communicates system and precision. Gut health has never looked like this, which is exactly the point.

**Colour palette:**

| Colour | Hex | Role |
|--------|-----|------|
| Warm amber | `#d4830a` | Primary background tone — warm, not medical, not food |
| Deep ochre / burnt sienna | `#8b4a1a` | Secondary warmth, depth |
| Off-white / cream | `#fdf4e3` | Title text — maximum contrast on warm background |
| Navy (brand anchor) | `#1b3a5c` | Author name, series name, structural accent — ties to interior brand |

**Compositional approach:**
1. **First:** the title — "The 7-Day Gut Reset" at maximum size, bold, compressed. The eye should read this before anything else, including at 160px thumbnail.
2. **Second:** a single, quiet visual element suggesting daily tracking — a partial view of a lined log page, a pen, or a minimal notebook texture. Not a full-bleed photograph. A graphic element or texture, not photography.
3. **Third:** subtitle and author name, in a smaller, quieter weight beneath the title.
4. The series name "Fix Your Gut for Good" appears at the top or bottom, small, Lato-weight — visible at full size, not competing with the title at thumbnail.

**Typography treatment:**
- **Title:** Bold, compressed or extended sans-serif — Bebas Neue, Barlow Condensed Bold, or Oswald Bold. All-caps. Maximum size the format allows. Cream `#fdf4e3` on amber background. Must pass the 160px squint test.
- **Subtitle:** "Find Your Trigger and Calm Your Gut in One Week" — 30–40% of title size. Lighter weight. May break across 2–3 lines. Same cream colour.
- **Author:** "S.A. Ibrahim" — smallest text. Navy `#1b3a5c` or cream, below subtitle.
- **Series brand:** "Fix Your Gut for Good" — Lato 300, small caps, muted, above the title or at the very bottom.

**What this cover is NOT doing:**
- No food photography of any kind — no bowls, plates, vegetables, fermented jars
- No digestive tract diagrams or gut anatomy illustrations
- No supplement bottles, pill bottles, powders
- No clinical blue or teal — zero
- No light, airy, wellness-spa aesthetic
- No script or handwritten fonts
- No stock photo human figure

## 9. WINNING CONCEPT — "THE SWITCH" (kooky direction, selected 2026-05-22)

**Brief from user:** "Extremely kooky and will definitely catch reader attention to purchase." Direction: bold, graphic, illustration-led — a design object, not a wellness book.
