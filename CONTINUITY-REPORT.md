Every book in the series is written to Sarah. This is non-negotiable.

- **Who she is:** 30s–40s woman, 2+ failed treatment cycles
- **Physical state:** Chronic bloating, fatigue, brain fog; symptoms spanning multiple body systems
- **Psychological state:** Doesn't trust her body anymore. Doesn't trust doctors to get it right. Opens the book not with hope — with last-ditch "maybe."

- **Thesis (from Book 1 — echoes through series):** "You didn't fail the treatment. The treatment failed to address the root cause."

## VOICE RULES — LOCKED FROM BOOK 1

### Sentence and paragraph structure
- Default sentence length: 12–18 words
- Science sentences: up to 25 words — must be immediately followed by a sentence of 8 words or fewer. The short sentence is the landing.
- Max 4 sentences per paragraph (science), 5 (narrative)
- Single-sentence paragraphs: encouraged when a point needs to land alone
- No wall-of-text ever

### Mandatory patterns (once per chapter minimum)
1. The Mirror Sentence — what Sarah is thinking right now, exactly
2. The Reframe Before the Information — WHY before rules or steps
3. The DCT Box (where relevant) — scripted sentences Sarah can copy to her notes app before an appointment; not "ask about your options"

### Banned language
- "It's important to note that" — if it's important, put it first
- "Some experts believe" — state the evidence or say you don't have it
- "Additionally" / "Furthermore" / "Moreover" — as paragraph openers
- "Healing journey" / "nourishing" / "clean eating"
- Moralizing about food in any form
- Medical caveats becoming vagueness

### Evidence language rules
- Level the evidence explicitly: RCT data vs. single study vs. mechanistic theory vs. preliminary trials
- Example from Book 1: ginger (Level 3, gastric emptying RCTs), Iberogast (Level 2, RCT-backed for functional dyspepsia), 5-HTP (Level 4/mechanistic theory only)
- Never treat preliminary evidence as settled
- Never soften uncertainty into implication
