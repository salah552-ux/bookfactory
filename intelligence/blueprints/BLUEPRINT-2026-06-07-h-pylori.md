## 2. AVATAR — "Sarah"

- **Demographic:** Woman, 30s–50s, UK or US, research-literate, Kindle-native, KU subscriber.
- **Trigger moment:** Has just tested positive for H. pylori (stool antigen or breath test), OR has just finished a course of triple/quadruple therapy and a re-test came back still positive.
- **Emotional state:** Anxious — specifically about stomach cancer. She has Googled "H. pylori cancer" and found the IARC Group 1 carcinogen classification and the 76%-of-gastric-cancers statistic, and is now frightened.
- **Frustration:** "The doctor gave me antibiotics and I'm still positive." Side effects from the antibiotics (metallic taste, nausea, diarrhoea). Feels dismissed — told "it's cleared, don't worry" without a re-test, or told to "just take another round."
- **What she wants:** (1) A clear plan to actually eradicate it, including what to do when first-line treatment fails; (2) reassurance grounded in real numbers about cancer risk (not panic, not dismissal); (3) a diet and stomach-lining-healing protocol; (4) NO supplement sales pitch — she is suspicious of "natural cure" sellers (the Matula Tea / Manuka Honey scam pattern is well known in the community).

**Voice anchor:** Mechanism over reassurance-theatre. Validate her fear with data, then defuse it with data. Never sell a supplement; cite the evidence and let her decide.

---

## 3. CORE PROMISE

A clear, evidence-grounded plan to eradicate H. pylori (including the second- and third-line options for when antibiotics fail), heal the stomach lining after the infection and after the antibiotics, and understand actual cancer risk in proportion — without panic and without a supplement sales pitch.
