  - BSR <50,000 ≈ 2–6 sales/day → 7 of 20 books (35%)

  - BSR >200,000 ≈ ≤1 sale/week → 8 of 20 books (40%)
- **Read:** Just under half the visible Top 20 are still moving real units. The other half are dead weight that the algorithm hasn't yet dropped. **A new launch that earns 30+ reviews can plausibly displace 7–8 of the books currently in the Top 20.**

### Two specific anomalies to interpret

**Anomaly A — BSR 8,091 with only 11 reviews ("Complete Guide to Taming Chronic Inflammation")**
This pattern signals one of:
1. Launch-window borrow surge (KU page-reads spiking BSR before review base catches up)
2. Active AMS spend converting cold traffic without leaving reviewers
3. A book that converts on KU pages-read economics, not on sale revenue

In all three cases the lesson is the same: **in this niche, ranking is achievable before reviews accumulate.** This validates the factory-mode strategy — push the book live, stack KU borrows, reviews follow rather than precede rank.

**Anomaly B — BSR 21,117 with 2 reviews (Spector's "Gut")**
This is the 8M-copy bestseller's UK paperback reissue. The "2 reviews" is an edition-specific count; the brand sells itself. **Do not treat this as a competitor — treat it as a halo that brings traffic into the category.** Our job is to be discovered in the "Customers also bought" trail behind Spector, not to fight him.

## 4. KU 100% Penetration — Real or Artifact?

100% KU penetration across 20 books is statistically improbable unless the scraper defaults to `true` when it can't confirm. The trad-pub anchors (Spector, Mosley, Enders) are almost certainly *not* in KU.

**Treat as:** **likely partial detection artifact, but pointing at a real signal.** The self-pub long tail is overwhelmingly KU-enrolled because that's where pages-read revenue lives in this niche.

**Strategy implication (unchanged either way):**
- Enrol all guides in KU Select for the first 90 days.
- A 5,500-word guide is ~22 KENPC pages → ~£0.10–£0.13 per full borrow at current KU rates.
- Ladder pricing (FREE → £0.99 → £1.99 → £2.99) maximises borrow velocity before pushing sale revenue.

## 5. Pricing — What the Spread Says

- Min £1.99, max £13.99, mean £8.10, sweet-spot £3.00.
- The £8.10 mean is dragged upward by paperback editions of the trad-pub anchors (£10–£14 print). Kindle self-pub clusters at £1.99–£3.99.
- The £3.00 sweet spot is the **self-pub Kindle peak conversion price** — exactly the ceiling KDP's 70% royalty band tops out (£2.99 = £2.09 royalty after delivery; £3.99 starts to suppress conversion).

**Recommended ladder for a 5,500-word short guide:**

| Stage | Trigger | Price | Why |
|-------|---------|-------|-----|
| Launch | Day 1–5 | FREE (KDP Free Promo) | Stack downloads + KU borrows + algorithm trust |
| Post-promo | Day 6–30 | £0.99 | Impulse-buy band; reviews accumulate |
| Mid-build | 10+ reviews OR BSR <150k | £1.99 | Royalty doubles, conversion still strong |
| Stable | 30+ reviews AND avg rating 4.3+ | £2.99 | Sweet spot; max royalty without conversion drop |

**Do not price above £2.99 for guides under 7,000 words** — pricing/length mismatch is the #1 review-bomb trigger in this category. Reader expectations at £3.99+ are book-length.

## 6. Content Gap Analysis — Apply Logic, Not Intuition

Three reported gaps. Scoring each on (a) demand validation, (b) competition density, (c) executability with the existing pipeline:

| Gap | Demand evidence | Existing entrants | Pipeline fit | Verdict |
|-----|-----------------|-------------------|--------------|---------|
| **Root cause** | Indirect — every top-seller skips diagnostics and jumps to protocol | Zero focused entrants in Top 20 | Strong — pipeline already produces SIBO/gut content | **Highest opportunity, lowest risk** |
| **Quick fix / time-bound** | Direct — "4-Week Protocol" (BSR 161k, 181 rev), "20-Minute Fix" (BSR 324k, 227 rev), "Super Gut Four-Week" (BSR 44k) | 3 validated entrants, demand confirmed | Strong — 5,500 words fits a 7-day arc | **Validated, but contested** |
| **Women-specific** | One entrant — "What Every Woman Needs to Know" (BSR 298k, 29 rev) | n=1, underperforming | Strong — pipeline can do female-specific health voice | **Ambiguous — could be small demand OR bad execution; not enough data** |

**Logic chain:**
- Root cause has the *cleanest* blue ocean but is unproven as a buy-trigger on its own.
- Quick fix has *proven* demand but is becoming crowded.
- Women-specific is a one-sample bet — cannot tell if the underperformance is the niche or the book.

**Optimal play:** **Fuse root cause + quick fix into a single hook.** Lead with the diagnostic ("find your trigger") to own the unfilled gap, then deliver the time-bound protocol that the market has already proven it will buy. The first product hits both validated demand and an empty angle in one SKU.

Women-specific becomes a **product #3 experiment** — build it cheap with the pipeline, see if the angle pulls. Don't bet the line on it.

## 7. The First Product

### Title
**The 7-Day Gut Reset: Find Your Trigger and Calm Your Gut in One Week**

Why this title:
- "7-Day" mirrors the proven time-bound pattern (4-Week, 20-Minute, Four-Week) but shortens to a more impulse-friendly commitment than the 4-week competitors.
- "Find Your Trigger" claims the empty root-cause angle without using the clinical word "diagnostic."
- "Calm Your Gut" — emotional payoff, not a medical claim. Compliance-clean.
- Keyword density: "gut" twice, "reset" once, "trigger" once, "week/day" twice — strong for KDP keyword field.

### Subtitle (back-up — A/B candidates)
1. *Find Your Trigger and Calm Your Gut in One Week*
2. *A Step-by-Step Plan to Identify the Foods Wrecking Your Gut — and Heal in 7 Days*
3. *The Beginner's Diagnostic-First Protocol for Bloating, Reflux, and IBS*

### Positioning
Every competitor jumps straight to "eat this, avoid that." This guide spends Days 1–2 helping the reader **identify their personal trigger** (food, stress, sleep, gut-brain axis), then Days 3–7 deliver a tailored 5-day calm-down protocol. Diagnostic-first, then prescriptive.
