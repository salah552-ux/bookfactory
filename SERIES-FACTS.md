## SERIES VOICE ANCHORS

Non-negotiable across every book in the series:

- **Tone:** Plainspoken · Unflinching · Warm-but-unsentimental · Quietly furious on the reader's behalf
- **Voice:** Second person ("you") throughout. Contractions always.
- **Author pen name:** S.A. Ibrahim — never disclosed as pen name in any book
- **Publisher:** Reflex Press
- **Structure:** 4-phase framework (Assess → Eradicate → Repair → Prevent Relapse) — this framework labels are Book 1-specific; future books adapt the spirit, not necessarily the exact phase names
- **Mirror Sentence:** One per chapter — exactly what the reader is thinking right now. Specific, not vague.
- **DCT Box:** Doctor Communication Toolkit sidebar in every relevant chapter. Scripted sentences, not "ask about your options."
- **NEVER:** "healing journey" · "nourishing" · "Furthermore" · "Additionally" · "Moreover" · "It is important to note" · "Some experts believe" · "clean eating"
- **NEVER moralize about food:** No "clean," "nourishing," "healing" food language
- **Sentence length default:** 12–18 words. Science sentences up to 25, immediately followed by a sentence of 8 words or fewer.
- **Paragraph max:** 4 sentences (science), 5 sentences (narrative). Single-sentence paragraphs encouraged when a point needs to land alone.
- **Jargon rule:** Define on first use in the same sentence. Use confidently after. No re-defining.
- **Evidence rule:** State the evidence or say you don't have it. Never "some experts believe."
- **Emotion rule:** Name it. Don't manage it. Acknowledge → validate briefly → pivot.
- **Target reader:** Sarah — 30s–40s woman, 2+ failed treatment cycles, doesn't trust her body anymore, opens book with last-ditch energy not hope

## CROSS-PROMOTION COPY BANK

Ready-to-insert mentions linking series books. Only use in books that are published. Check SERIES-ROADMAP.md for current publication status.

**SIBO → Leaky Gut (Book 2):**
> If your SIBO keeps coming back despite treatment, intestinal permeability — what most people call leaky gut — is often the structural reason why. The two conditions are tightly linked. *Fix Your Gut for Good — Leaky Gut* covers the mechanism and the repair protocol in full.

**SIBO → SIFO (Book 3):**
> Fungal overgrowth in the small intestine — SIFO — is SIBO's less-discussed sibling. The two frequently co-exist, and treating one without addressing the other is a common reason people cycle through treatment without getting well. *Fix Your Gut for Good — SIFO* covers it in depth.

**SIBO → Microbiome Reset (Book 10):**
> Clearing SIBO is not the same as rebuilding your microbiome. Once the overgrowth is gone, the work of restoring genuine microbial diversity begins. *Fix Your Gut for Good — Microbiome Reset* picks up exactly where this book ends.
